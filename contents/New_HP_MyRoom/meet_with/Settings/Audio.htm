Audio/Video

Adjust your speakers and microphone for best audio quality in your room. Click on the Settings icon to bring up the Audio/Video window and test your sound and webcam. Note that when you are testing, you cannot be heard in the room but you cannot hear others talking in the room either.

 

NOTE: PulseAudio is required for Linux systems using MyRoom version 10.9 and higher. Go to the Sound settings on your Linux system. Explicitly select your audio device in both the Output and Input tabs. Verify your microphone volume is not set too low or muted. Test your device in Sound settings. Then proceed to test and adjust your settings in MyRoom as below.

 

In many PCs there may be more than one available microphone input as well as more than one speaker/headset option.        

 

 

If you have and need to use two cameras, tick the Use second camera option and then choose your primary and secondary cameras from the drop down lists.  

  

   

Select the microphone and speakers you are using, adjust the volumes, then click on Start test. Start speaking to verify the selections and, if necessary, adjust the volume for microphone and speakers. You will see the audio graph in the top section as you are speaking into the microphone. Once you are satisfied with the volumes, click Stop test and then either choose another Settings menu item or close the window using the red X.  

 

While the A/V test is running, you will not be seen or heard by others in the same room.

   

NOTE: MacOS has new security and privacy features that may affect your new installation of MyRoom on the Mac. If you experience difficulties with your microphone and camera such as microphone and camera not working for your test as described above, or if you have difficulty sharing your screen and you have answered Yes to any request to enable audio and video, follow these steps to enable audio and video and sharing for your Mac:

  1. Click on the Apple Menu.

  2. Choose System Preferences.

  3. Launch the Security & Privacy option.

  4. Click on the lock at the bottom to make changes.

  5. In the left column, click on Camera.

  6. In the right column, select HPE MyRoom to tick the box.

  7. If you have already launched MyRoom, you will be asked to quit HPE MyRoom to complete the access to your camera. Choose Later.

   

  1. In the left column, click on Microphone.

  2. In the right column, select HPE MyRoom to tick the box.

  3. If you have already launched MyRoom, you will be asked to quit HPE MyRoom to complete the access to your microphone. Choose Later.

   

  1. In the left column, click on Screen recording.

  2. In the right column, select HPE MyRoom to tick the box.

  3. If you have already launched MyRoom, you will be asked to quit HPE MyRoom to complete the process. Choose Quit Now.

  4. Launch HPE MyRoom.

NOTE: If any of the options are already checked, you may have to toggle them off and on again. Remember to log out of MyRoom to commit these changes.

When you click on the A/V settings help icon (v),you see the page at right, explaining the various options available for the audio/video section.  

 

 

When you right click on the A/V settings help icon, another option appears so you can choose the video resolution you want to use. Check your attendees' network connection in Audience details (right click in the People panel, choose Show audience details) to see five bars for each and then change your video resolution to HD resolution for higher quality video. Be aware that using the HD option uses more bandwidth for each attendee..
