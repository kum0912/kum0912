More details about MyRoom connection health for the technically inclined

The MyRoom connection health indicator in the upper right of your chat or room shows the status of your connection to MyRoom during the chat or meeting. You can also see this indicator in the Audience details window for others in the room. The MyRoom connection health indicator has five bars.
 

Error rate is the percentage of packets that needed to be re-transmitted. This typically occurs when your computer does not receive an acknowledgement from the MyRoom server within an expected amount of time. Your computer assumes the packet has been lost and retransmits it. There needs to be at least five packets transmitted to calculate an error rate.
Number of Bars Status Error rate health
5 Excellent < 2%
4 Good < 3%
3 Adequate < 6%
2 Marginal < 10%
1 Bad >= 10%
   
Round trip time is the time from sending a packet to receiving an acknowledgement. The size of these packets may vary which affects the variance of the average round trip time calculations.
Number of Bars Status Round trip time health
5 Excellent < 300 milliseconds
4 Good < 500 milliseconds
3 Adequate < 800 milliseconds
2 Marginal < 1000 milliseconds
1 Bad 1 second or more
   
Note that both error rates and round trip times are calculated from the MyRoom user's perspective (you or the person you are meeting with). A user may simply be receiving data without sending anything (e.g., listening to someone speak). In this case, there is no basis for calculating network health so instead, pings are sent. Pings also have a round trip time, but the packets are always of minimal size so variance in the average ping time calculations should be minimal.
Number of Bars Status Ping time health
5 Excellent < 100 milliseconds
4 Good < 300 milliseconds
3 Adequate < 500 milliseconds
2 Marginal < 1000 milliseconds
1 Bad 1 second or more
   
Currently, if there is traffic (i.e., no need to resort to pings), MyRoom connection health is calculated from the protocol (TCP or UDP) with the most traffic by taking the minimum of error rate health and round trip health.  
   
Troubleshooting for HPE or HPI employees

For employees using an HP Folio 1020 or 1040, you can set up a wired connection using the HP Side Dock/VGA adapter (G7U78AA) available in SmartBuy.

 

  1. Use the highest bandwidth network connection available to you.

 

If you are connected to the HP network remotely:

 

  • The recommended VPN solution is HP DirectAccess since it does not require a special set-up.
  • If you are connected to the HP network using Pulse VPN, do not select entries that have "split tunnel disabled." This can lead to poor audio quality.

  • The last option is HP Remote Access 6.5. Please ensure that Disable Split Tunnel is unchecked.
  • If you experience issues making calls at home it could be due to your network connectivity. The help desk does not support home networks due to all the complexities of third-part modems, routers and other equipment. It is virtually impossible to troubleshoot every manufacturer's home-based hardware.
  • If using wireless, connect your PC to your router using a LAN/Ethernet cable. Interference can degrade the wireless signal between your PC and your wireless access point. Connect your PC to the router using a LAN/Ethernet cable for the best possible network connection.
  • If using a router, connect your PC directly to your modem using a LAN/Ethernet cable. Home routers can apparetly either block the traffic by a setting in the router's menu or the router has older firmware that does not allow the traffic to pass through. To properly test your home connections, connect your notebook PC directly to the cable/DSL/Fios modem and make a test call to a colleague or teammate. If the call goes through, your home router is blocking the traffic. If your cable/DSL/Fios modem has a built-in router, you must turn off NAT (DHCP) and allow your PC to get an IP address directly from your internet provider. Your must perform the test with no router between your PC and the internet.
