Play a recording

As soon as you click on the Stop button in the Record Control window, your recording is saved to your My Documents folder or the default location you specify in Settings/File handling and the Recording finished window appears. You can also install a Webm player, play and upload your video,  and open the file location and send the recording by email.

 

Recordings are now automatically saved in the MP4 format as of MyRoom 12.7. If you are on version 12.6 without the MP4 extension, you will not be able to add the extension and will be unable to record and will need to upgrade to version 12.7 to do so. If you have the MP4 extension on version 12.6 you will be able to record in MP4. If you then uninstall the extension, you will not be able to reinstall it, you would need to upgrade your version of MyRoom. 

 

You can now play your recording. If you are using Internet Explorer and need to install a .webm player, click on Click to install player and follow the on-screen instructions to install the player.    

 

 
Click Play to play your recording. Your default media player opens and the recording plays.

 

 
You can upload the recording to the Content area of the room by choosing the Upload option. The file is saved with a filename in the format RecMM-DD-YYYY_HH-MM-SS.webm. You can also move the file to a Handouts folder so that Participants can download and play the file.  

 

 
Click Save copy as to save an additional copy of your recording to a local or network location of your choice.

 

 
Clicking Open folder takes you to your My Documents folder or the default location you specify in Settings/File handling where your recording has been saved.  

 

 
You can send the video by clicking Email. Your email application will create a formatted email with the recording file already attached and standard default text telling the recipient how to play the file. Add email addresses and send the email.

 

 
For those with versions of MyRoom older than 12.7 who are recording in the WebM format, click on Help - Viewing this video to launch a webpage regarding playing Webm video.
