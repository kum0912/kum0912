Start a chat with audio

Both people in a chat can use audio, if their computer has a microphone and speakers or they have headphones.

 

Both see the Call button in the top under the name of the person you are chatting with. You can start the chat by typing in the chat area or, if you want to speak to the other person, click the Call button.

   
The other person in the chat receives the message at right.

   
Until the call is accepted, the caller sees this.

   
If the other person clicks on Reject call, you as the initiator of the call will see this.

   
Once a call is accepted or when you add others to the chat, the Call button changes to the Microphone, Speaker and Video buttons, with a small box reminding you that the Microphone is muted. Click the microphone icon to unmute. You can tick the box Don't show this again to avoid seeing the message in the future. The webcam can be clicked if you want to use video as well.   NOTE: PulseAudio is required for Linux systems using MyRoom version 10.9 and higher.

   
You can hover over the microphone and speaker icons to adjust the volume for each or change your video location using the webcam icon. To make adjustments to the microphone, speaker and webcam,  click on the Settings icon and go to Audio/Video.
