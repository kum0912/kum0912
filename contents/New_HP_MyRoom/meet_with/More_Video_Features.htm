More video features

You can use webcams in HPE MyRoom to make your meeting or event more personal and interactive. Everyone who joins a chat controls their own webcam. Participants in an event have only a speaker by default. They can be given access to the microphone and webcam tools through the Configure room for participants choice under the Tools to manage people and room features button . In an event, all webcams can be turned on and the screen will change to accommodate the number of videos to be displayed so the more webcams that are turned on, the smaller each video will be. Video quality will be determined by the number of webcams running and the quality of connectivity for each person.

 

Turn on the webcam by clicking the icon in the top right corner.

   

Hover over the webcam icon to see the video position/video layout drop down menu. The default view is Center with videos displayed in the center viewing area and the Gallery layout.

 

Changes you make to the video position persist across allyour rooms until you change it again.

 

NOTE: These video layouts are not applied when a whiteboard is showing in center stage. In that case, floating videos over the whiteboard layout is used.

   

Choose Video: Left to move all videos to the left and leave the center area clear.

 

Once you have made your choice, that choice persists across all your rooms until you change it again.

 

 

   

Choose Video: Popup to detach the videos from the main meeting room. You can move the popup window to a second screen if you have one so that you can see both the videos and the meeting room at the same time as in the example at right.

 

Once you have made your choice, that choice persists across all your rooms until you change it again.

 

 

   

You can also choose your video layout preference for your videos from the dropdown menu. The default view is Gallery layout with a grid layout of videos which depends on the number of videos displayed. As more videos are started, the size of each gets smaller. Each person in the room makes their own choice for the video layout that does not impact that of others in the room.

 

NOTE: These video layouts are not applied when a whiteboard is showing in center stage. In that case, floating videos over the whiteboard layout is used.
   

When you choose Speaker from the dropdown menu, who you see in the center viewing area depends on who is speaking. As a different person starts speaking, their video will display above the others in the center area. Each person in the room makes their own choice for the video layout that does not impact that of others in the room.

 

NOTE: These video layouts are not applied when a whiteboard is showing in center stage. In that case, floating videos over the whiteboard layout is used.
   

When you choose Pinned from the dropdown menu, your own video is automatically pinned to the center area and will stay there until you change your choice. When you hover over and then click on the Pin video icon  on another video than your own, that video will be pinned to the center area and will stay there until you change your choice. The pinned video is indicated by the different direction of the pin in the icon .  Each individual can anchor a specific video by pinning it. Each person in the room makes their own choice for the video layout that does not impact that of others in the room.

 

NOTE: These video layouts are not applied when a whiteboard is showing in center stage. In that case, floating videos over the whiteboard layout is used.
   
Once you have clicked the webcam icon and your video appears, there are two options you can choose: pause your own video and snap your own video. Participants who have been given cameras can also pause their videos.
