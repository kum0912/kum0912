MyRoom connection health indicator

The MyRoom connection health indicator in the upper right of your chat or room shows the status of your MyRoom connection during the chat or meeting. This health indicator is based on pings from your device to MyRoom servers and back again. You can also see this indicator in the Audience details window for others in the room. The MyRoom connection health indicator has five bars.  

 

This option is not turned on by default in your rooms, either a chat or event room. Go to the Settings/General tab and click Show network metrics to display this option.

A value of zero (all five bars gray) means there is not enough information to determine the MyRoom connection health. This will occur when you first enter a room. It will also happen if you lose your network connection and are unable to connect to the internet. Each bar will turn from gray to white to indicate the strength of the connection as at right.  
Number of Bars Icon Status
5 Excellent
4 Good
3 Adequate
2 Marginal
1 Bad
0 No connection
   

When your MyRoom connection health indicator shows 1 bar repeatedly or for a prolonged period of time, you may experience some of the following:

 

  1. Poor audio quality: voices you hear may sound tinny or unnatural.

  2. Loss of words: portions of the sound may be lost.

  3. Delayed speech: there is a long delay between when one person speaks (as seen in your People panel speech indicator) and when you hear them.

  4. Audio (sound) and video (live streaming video) are out of sync.

  5. Annotations appearing before slides.

  6. Downloading indicator for a long period of time.
   

Poor MyRoom connection health may be caused by one or more of the following:

 

  • Limited bandwidth
  • Network bandwidth between your PC and the MyRoom servers is limited. This restriction could occur anywhere in your network connection such as your home network, your Internet Service Provider (ISP) and the local area network (LAN) in your office. Bandwidth intensive applications running on the network such as file downloads or streaming video can also limit your bandwidth.
    • NOTE:  If you are working from home, remember others in your home may be sharing your bandwidth, limiting the available bandwidth for your session.
  • To test your bandwidth, perform a bandwidth test such as Speed test. This will validate that the measured bandwidth is what you expect based on the service you are subscribed to from your ISP.
  • Especially on a home network, try to minimize any other bandwidth intensive applications such as those mentioned above during your chat or meeting.
  • High Latency
  • There is significant delay in the time it takes for data to be sent from your PC and received by the destination system, in this case the MyRoom servers. This can occur if the network that your PC is connected to is physically located far from the MyRoom servers. Latency can also occur if a delay is introduced at one of the network hops between your PC and the MyRoom servers.
  • Test for latency using a tool like the one found here: Latency test. Your ping time latency should be <500 milliseconds.
  • You can optimize any latency by, among other things, closing open programs, renewing your computer's IP address and verifying your network protocols. There are many resources on the internet that can assist you in minimizing latency such as this one.
  • Wireless LAN interference
  • Wireless (or Wi-Fi) network connections are not as strong as wired (Ethernet) connections due to the possibility of interference with the wireless signal or low signal strength. Other devices that might operate on the same radio frequency can cause interference. Low signal strength can occur when the PC is far from the wireless access point or there are walls or solid objects between the PC and the access point.
  • To improve performance, try moving the wireless access point closer to the PC (or vice versa) or plug directly into a LAN for a wired connection.
  • Make sure your home router and modem have the latest firmware.
  • Home networks
  • Home networks introduce added complexity when you use of a variety of network equipment (routers, hubs, switches) from a variety of vendors and a variety of broadband service offerings (DSL, cable and fiber) from a variety of vendors.
  • Hardware in your home, at the ISP, or in an office may be misconfigured. If possible, try another device, such as a different modem, router, hub or switch, and ensure that all these devices have the latest firmware. Sometimes, rebooting your equipment will help.
  • On a wired network, try the following:
    • Disable the wireless (WiFi) network adapter.
    • Temporarily stop using bandwidth-intensive activities such as file downloads and streaming videos.
    • Check that LAN cables are well seated and in good condition.
    • Make sure your home router or modem has the latest firmware.
    • Dial-up networking is the least reliable and produces the poorest audio quality.

 

Suggestions for better audio quality

In addition to the above, following the guidelines below should optimize your audio experience in MyRoom.

 

  1. Do not use the PC microphone and speakers. This produces the worst audio quality.
  2. Only plug your headset into your PC or the powered USB ports on your docking station. Docking stations may have only one powered USB port. If you don't know if your docking station has a powered USB port, plug the headset into a USB port on the PC.
  3. Running multiple applications that are CPU or memory-intensive can affect audio quality. When possible, close unnecessary applications or processor-intensive applications when you are in an HPE MyRoom room.
  4. Confirm that the person you are speaking to is also following the steps above.
  5. The meeting leader should mute the lines of participants generating noise and disrupting the conference. Review the information regarding managing large MyRoom meetings. Meeting participants should mute their microphones when not speaking.
  6. If you have trouble with your audio device, contact the device manufacturer for support:
