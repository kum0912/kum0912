Request remote control

In a meeting or chat, those with HPE MyRoom Assist and HPE MyRoom University can request remote control of another person's desktop. This is useful for demonstrations, how-to scenarios and training sessions, as well as for technical support. You can take control back with one click of a button. More than one remote control session can take place at a time in a room. Request remote control is part of the HPE MyRoom Assist and the HPE MyRoom University add-ons you can purchase here.

 

NOTE: At this time, typing directly on a remote Mac or Linux machine may not work as expected. As a workaround for the present, from Windows, use the Copy/Paste function here to transfer commands or other text from or to another operating system such as Mac or Linux.

 

Right click on the name of the person you are requesting control from or choose Tools to manage people and room features and choose Request Remote Control.

 

 
The other person receives this message.

   
If the other person clicks No, a message similar to the one at right appears. Click OK to exit from the message.

   

A sharing window now opens showing the other person's desktop. You can now control the other person's desktop.  That person can also control the mouse so if the mouse appears to be moving without any purpose, it is probably because both of you are moving the mouse at the same time. You can release control by clicking the Release control button in the lower right hand corner of the shared window.
