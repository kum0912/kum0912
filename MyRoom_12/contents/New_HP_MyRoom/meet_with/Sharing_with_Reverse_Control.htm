Grant control

In a meeting or chat, those with HPE MyRoom Assist and HPE MyRoom University can grant control of their desktop to another person. Grant control allows you as a Presenter to hand over control of your shared region to another person in your meeting. This is useful for demonstrations, how-to scenarios and training sessions, as well as for technical support. You can take control back with one click of a button. Grant control is part of the HPE MyRoom Assist add-on you can purchase here.

 

NOTE: New macOS Mojave security and privacy features may affect MyRoom on the Mac. If you experience difficulties with  granting remote control on your macOS Mojave machine, please follow these steps to enable this for your Mac:

  • Launch and unlock the Security & Privacy option in System Preferences.

  • In the left column, click on Accessibility to make changes to granting remote control.

  • In the right column, select HPE MyRoom to tick the box. Choose Later if you are asked to quit MyRoom.

  • Click the lock at the bottom to prevent further changes.

  • If you have already launched MyRoom, log out to apply your changes and then launch HPE MyRoom again.

 

If your account has the Grant control option, once you have started sharing you will see the Transfer control to... icon in the Share control window.

 

 
Clicking on the Transfer control to: button brings up a list of all users in the room. Choose one name and click on Assign.

   
The other person is advised of your transfer of control. That person must click Yes to accept or No to refuse remote control. Once control is accepted, both parties are in control of one desktop and both can move the mouse, click on objects, etc., at the same time. If the mouse appears to be moving without any purpose, it is probably because you are both moving the mouse at the same time.

   
To take back control from another person, click on the Take back control button in the Share Control window. Control is returned to the original Presenter immediately.
