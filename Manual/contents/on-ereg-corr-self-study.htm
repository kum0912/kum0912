Correspondence/self-study/e-learning

 

The Correspondence/self-study/e-learning option provides Ontario schools and boards with the ability to generate the official Register of Enrolment in Correspondence/self-Study/e-Learning Courses. This register option is available to Ontario boards and schools set up with ‘school operating type’ of continuing education. Occasionally, a board will allow a CE student to register in a correspondence/self-study/e-learning course at a summer school. Therefore, this option is also available for summer schools so that these CE students can print on this register rather than the Summer School Register.

 

This register records data on pupil’s lessons/work units marked (not attendance) only for pupils enrolled in the following courses:

 

  • Correspondence courses offered using materials provided by the ILC  
  • Correspondence or self-study courses prepared by the board  
  • E-learning courses prepared by the board  
  • E-learning offered through the provincial LMS

 

The courses must be provided:

 

  • During the school year,  
  • In the summer, or  
  • From September to August

  

This register may include pupils exclusively enrolled in CE, part-time day pupils. Full-time day pupils taking CE correspondence/self-study/e-learning courses will appear on the register, however their lessons/work units marked will not be funded unless the course was taken in the summer period. If the full-time day pupil is to receive this funding as well, set the student’s Con. Ed. Student Type to ‘Also attends day school’.  

 

Schools and boards should be familiar with the official Ministry enrolment register instructions for continuing education programs. These instructions are updated annually on the Ministry’s site http://www.edu.gov.on.ca/eng/policyfunding/forms.html. This includes detailed information on which courses to include on this register.

 

Please be familiar with the Enrolment > Enrolment register info option in the student’s record. This will provide detailed information on the enrolment register(s) the student will appear on, as well as high credit and regular FTE/ADE info. Hint: on the FTE and/or ADE card in the student’s record, there’s a Details section that may be expanded to show info on how FTE/ADE is calculated, and how the student’s data impacts the register. Correspondence, self-study & e-learning classes are reported as ADE.

 

References to ‘lessons’ refers to ‘lessons/work units’.

 

Set up classes that need to appear on the Register of Correspondence/Self-Study/e-Learning Courses

If a class is to appear in the Correspondence/Self-Study/e-Learning register, it must be set up (Setup > Class info > Classes) as follows:

 

  • The course offering type must be set to one of the following:

- Independent Learning Centre (ILC) course

- Correspondence course

- e-Learning course *

 

  • Enter the most appropriate Delivery and Course delivery type (* e-learning must have a summer delivery in order to appear on this register).

  • Record the number of lessons that teachers must mark. This number is not to exceed 20 per credit.
  • If the class has start and end dates, record those dates, otherwise the class may be flagged as continuous entry (runs all year and each student can start and finish the course at their pace).

  

Teachers track marked lessons/work units

Since funding allocation is based on the number of lessons/work units marked, rather than attendance, the teacher will need to track the marked lessons/work units by entering a ‘mark’ when it is marked (does not need to be successfully completed to be recognized for grant purposes). Any mark other than zero will be considered a marked lesson/work unit on the register.  

 

The teacher will have a markbook category called ‘Lessons Marked’, with markbook items L1, L2 … up to the # of lessons set up for the class.  This is an example of what the teacher will see:

 

 

Teachers may want to change the titles from Lesson 1, etc, to a title that reflects the course material in the unit.  Since students may complete units at different times or different orders, the date the mark was entered is automatically recorded and will appear beside the mark as shown here:

 

 

The teacher may select this date and change it.  Teachers should be making sure this date accurately represent the student’s lesson date.  For students who received full-time funding on the October and/or March count date, the student’s lesson date is used to determine whether the lesson is included (funded) on the register.

 

Generate the Register of Enrolment in Correspondence/Self-Study/e-Learning Courses

To access the register, select Ontario reporting > Enrolment registers > Correspondence/self-study/e-learning.

 

The form will look like this:  

 

 

The list of classes will default any classes that are set up to appear on this register. You may limit the classes using the filters, or select an individual or range of classes to print.  A separate register is generated for each selected class.

 

Select the Print button located at the bottom of the form. Once the report has been submitted to the task queue, you may leave this application or generate other reports. Lessons eligible for funding

 

All lessons on the class list details page are not necessarily eligible for funding.  Some lessons may be subtracted as recorded in the “Subtract: lessons for full-time day school pupils” field.

 

 

Lessons are recognized for funding if the student’s Con. Ed. Student Type is:

 

  • Only Attends Con. Ed. School
  • Also Attends Day School (Full-time)  
  • Also Attends Day School (Part-time)

 

If a student is an “Other Pupil”, their lessons will not be recognized for funding (their lessons are subtracted in the ‘lessons for full-time pupils’ section).  

 

Some students are not eligible for funding and their lessons taught between September 1 and June 30 must be subtracted.  When a student’s Con. Ed Type is one of the following, their lessons are subtracted:

 

  • Full-Time Day School (exclude from register totals)
  • Part-time taking same course(s) in day school (exclude from register totals).

 

All lessons are funded for pupils of the board if they are between July 1 and August 31 (based on the date each student’s lesson was marked), regardless of the student’s Con. Ed. Type.

 

Since students may change Con. Ed. Types throughout the school year, depending upon the date of the student's Con. Ed. Student Type change (as recorded in the student’s internal transfers), some lessons may be counted. Using an example of a student who changes from Only Attends Con. Ed. School (which is funded on this CE register) to Full-time Day school (which is not funded on this CE register), the date of the change will impact the sessions counted as follows:

 

  • Internal transfer date is prior to 10/31:  it will exclude all lessons (because the student received FT funding on the OnSIS count Oct 31/Mar 31 dates).
  • Internal transfer date is after 10/31 and before March 31: it will count all lessons between Sept 1 and Jan 31 (and then the student will receive FT funding on the OnSIS Mar 31 count date).
  • Internal transfer date is after March 31 date: it will count all lessons (the student does not get FT funding for either Oct or March, but receives the CE funding instead).

 

Funding - OnSIS reporting of classes on the Continuing Education Registers

Funding is based on the information submitted to OnSIS.  See OnSIS > Night/continuing education school > ConEd/Night school info for details.

 

From the Ministry’s instructions:

 

Elemental data is collected once a year on August 31 for the entire school year (September-August). Submit data through the Continuing Education and/or Summer School submissions (School Special Condition Type “N – Continuing Education” or “8 – Summer”).  

 

Course Offering Type must be reported for each course for each student. Course Offering Types include:

 

  • Independent Learning Centre (ILC) course

  • Correspondence Course

  • E-Learning Course

 

 

The following Class Enrolment fields are mandatory:

 

  •  Course Schedule Indicator  

  •  Course Delivery  

  •  Course Language of Instruction

  •  Course Code  

  •  Course Start Date  

  •  Course End Date  

  •  Attempted Credit Value  

  •  Earned Credit Value  

  •  Final Mark  

 

The corresponding ADE value must also be reported for each course in the “ADE – September to June” or “ADE – Summer” fields.

 

ADE calculations for classes on the Correspondence/Self-Study/e-Learning Courses registers

The calculation of the average daily enrolment (ADE) is calculated as follows:

 

ADE = # of lessons eligible for funding x 0.00579 (rounded to four decimals)
