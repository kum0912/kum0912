Attendance > Enter attendance > Homeroom

 

 

Overview

Homeroom attendance may apply to your school, depending upon your school’s setup. For example,

 

  • School uses the ‘by period’ attendance method: A school has a designated homeroom time where students meet prior to dispersing to the classes in their timetables, and the schools want to track the students attendance in this homeroom period (as indicated in the setup of the school’s attendance parameters).
  • School uses the morning/afternoon attendance method: The school has assigned students into homerooms corresponding to their core teacher, and that the teacher is responsible for recording attendance for the group of students in his/her homeroom(s).

 

Ideally, the teacher takes attendance immediately and enters it directly into edsembli CONNECT.

 

From the school office perspective, the Homeroom option is used in the following situations:

 

  • Teachers do not have access to edsembli CONNECT in the classroom so attendance is recorded on paper and delivered to the office for entry.  
  • Regardless of who enters the attendance, the office will need to monitor that attendance has been recorded for all homerooms. Once this is done, the office typically follows up on students with unexplained absences.

 

From the main menu, choose Attendance then Enter attendance. This will take you to the Enter attendance section of the Attendance page where you will find the Homeroom card:

 

 

Select the GO button to access the homeroom attendance page.

 

Homeroom attendance

The office can use the homeroom attendance page to,

 

  • find homerooms that have not yet taken attendance,
  • Enter homeroom attendance if teacher cannot.

 

The homeroom attendance page starts with a date field to set the attendance taking date. Today’s date will be defaulted, but you can change the date. Below the date will be a card containing the homerooms in the school as shown here:

 

 

Find homerooms that have not yet taken attendance

If the teachers enter homeroom attendance directly from the classroom, the school office will access the homeroom attendance page to see what teachers have not yet entered attendance. If the Entered column shows No, that indicates homeroom has not yet entered attendance.

 

The office has the ability to show or hide the homerooms that have already had attendance taken. One of the following 2 icons will appear in the toolbar to indicate whether or not the list shows or hides those homerooms that have already had attendance entered. Selecting one icon to changes to the other icon.

 

 List does not include homerooms that have attendance taken. If all homerooms have had attendance taken, the list of homerooms will be empty.

 

List includes homerooms that have attendance taken.

 

If the attendance hasn't been taken, the office can remind the teachers, if necessary, to complete their attendance so that the office can start tracking down the students with unexplained attendance.

 

Special note to schools where attendance is entered by homeroom more than once a day: Once homeroom attendance is taken at the start of the day, the attendance is considered taken for the day. If your school repeats the homeroom attendance process later in the day, then the office can select the reset attendance icon before teachers commence the next round of attendance entries. All homerooms will be set back to ‘not taken’. This icon is found in the tool bar above the list of homerooms.

Enter homeroom attendance if teacher cannot

If a teacher is unable to enter homeroom attendance directly from the classroom, then the school office will access the homeroom attendance page to enter the attendance information.

 

From the list of homerooms, select the homeroom for which you want to enter attendance. The selected homeroom will appear in the title of the second card on the page. This card will include the students currently assigned to that homeroom.

 

For each student in the list, record attendance by selecting from the list of attendance codes. See Important concepts – Attendance codes and reasons for details. You can also enter Attendance notes.

 

There is a Bulk apply feature available for office users to assist in the entry of the same attendance codes for multiple students. See Bulk apply for information on this feature.

 

Below is an example of the entry of homeroom attendance where all students are the morning/afternoon method. You can enter the attendance information by selecting the appropriate attendance code from the morning and/or afternoon attendance fields provided for each student. If a student’s attendance type is morning only, then he/she will only have the morning attendance field.

 

 

If a school uses the ‘by period’ attendance method, and they take attendance in a homeroom period, only one attendance entry is required for each student, corresponding to the student’s attendance in the homeroom period.

 

 

Although this would rarely happen, if a homeroom has students with both methods of attendance, tabs for MORNING/AFTERNOON and BY PERIOD will appear. Students in the homeroom will appear under the tab corresponding to their attendance method.
