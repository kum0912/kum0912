OnSIS overview

 

Overview

The Ontario Ministry of Education's province-wide OnSIS (Ontario School Information System) captures data at the elemental and aggregate levels from schools and boards. The data collected provides accurate enrolment data to support the Ministry’s funding formula. In addition, it provides a foundation for future initiatives and educational programs, and facilitates in-depth analysis and reporting within the Ministry.  

 

The Ministry of Education has an OnSIS library which includes User Guides and Training Manuals. Once you log into the Ministry’s OnSIS application, this documentation is available by clicking the What’s New button. Here you will find detailed information on the Ministry’s application, and it is a good starting point for anyone unfamiliar to OnSIS. Refer to the Ministry’s documentation for the most accurate instructions on how to use their application. Any instructions provided by edsembli are guidelines only.

 

 

 

Using the edsembli SIS, schools and boards may generate batch files which can be submitted to the Ministry’s OnSIS application so that the student and school data doesn’t need to be manually entered into OnSIS. The batch files may be created in the SIS either by the individual schools, or for multiple schools by the board.  

 

OnSIS collects more than student data, and therefore other software applications may be used to maintain data that is not part of the SIS. For example, OnSIS may collect personnel information that may be maintained in a Human Resources (HR) application. The SIS batch files are able to provide OnSIS information regarding teachers’ homeroom/class/subject assignments, but other educator information must be updated in OnSIS either manually or through an OnSIS compatible HR application.

 

Count dates

OnSIS captures the data in the schools as of specific count dates. Depending on the type of school and the submission required of the schools, the count dates, as of the current school year, are as follows:

 

  • October 31
  • March 31
  • June 30
  • August 31

 

When the count date arrives, the new submission period is ‘opened’ for the school if the previous submission is signed off in OnSIS. The data collected by OnSIS is updated through the process of creating batch files using the live school data, and the school is able to continue updating the data as they normally would, providing they sign off the submissions in a timely manner. If the school has rolled over to a new school year, backups may be required if they have not finished the previous year’s OnSIS submissions.  

 

The batch files will calculate the student’s data as of the count date. For example, when a student changes from full-time to part-time, the date of the change is recorded in the student’s internal transfers allowing the SIS to determine whether to report the student as FT or PT on the count date).  

 

Submission periods

Each school will have one or more submission periods that are required for OnSIS. These submission periods will vary depending on whether it is an elementary or secondary school, and whether it is a publicly funded school or an inspected or non-inspected private school. Publicly funded regular schools have different requirements from continuing education and summer schools, and publicly funded regular schools may also be configured to require continuing education submission periods.
