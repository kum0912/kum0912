Fees overview

 

 

Every school collects money (funds) from students for various reasons. Funds include all forms of payment, from cash to cheque to e-payments. How these funds are collected and recorded may vary by school.  The Fees area is an excellent time saving feature that allows schools to record financial transactions for both current and past students. It has been designed as an Accounts Receivable for student and beyond data entry there exist very powerful reporting features. Schools are strongly encouraged to involve their financial resources during the setup process. Additionally, it is important to plan ahead of time just how the Accounts Receivable for students will best benefit the students, school, division and community at large.

It is recommended that a school Setup their Accounts area prior to Assigning Fees and/or generating one of the many available Reports. Prior to the getting into the setup, it is useful to become familiar with some of the terminology that is used throughout the Accounts Area.

Transactions - Current vs Archives  

Each time an entry is recorded in the fees area for a student, it is recorded as a transaction. Transactions occur when a fee is added, a payment or deposit is made, a refund is issued, or a payment is reversed due to non-sufficient funds (NSF cheque).

 

There are two distinct areas where transactions are stored. To distinguish between these 2 areas, they are referred to as current and archives.

 

Current Transactions

Current Transactions refers to any activity with respect to Accounts for all Current Students. This does not include Past Students.

 

Archived Transactions

In some instances, schools like to only see outstanding transactions for students. For example, moving from year to year using the Archive Utility a school may wish to move all zero balances to the archive table of the database. Please note, partial payments will keep all tied transactions in the Current Transactions table. The decision to Archive Transactions or not is one that is dependent upon the school/board accounting policy. With the exception of moving students to past (done at the end of the year with the Transfer to New Year process), Maplewood does not automatically Archive Transactions. An advantage to Archiving Transactions is a concise display on the students’ accounts tab as well as increased performance on the accounts database.

 

A student’s transactions will remain as current, until one of the following happens:

 

1. The student’s status changes to Past. As part of the year-end process, all students who are not returning to the school automatically become past students and their fees are all moved to the archives. Any outstanding fees may be paid or edited as required from the ARCHIVES tab in the student’s fee function. The student’s Account Bill may also be printed from the fees area of the student’s record.

 

2. The school runs a tool that archives transactions prior to a specific date. This process transfers the current transactions for all students to the archives. Some schools run this tool at the end of each school year, while others never run it.  This means that current transactions may extend beyond the current year if the school does not run the archive process. If a student has an outstanding fee (where the fee is not yet fully paid), that fee will not be archived. As part of the archive process, account bills may be automatically generated for those students with outstanding balances.

 

Some key concepts to understand:

 

  • When looking at a student’s fees, you are looking at the student’s current transactions, unless you navigate to the ARCHIVES tab where you will find the archived transactions. You are looking at current transactions when you are on the SUMMARY , DEPOSITS SUMMARY, and TRANSACTIONS tabs.
  • New fees can be added, and payments/refunds/voids can be made for both current and archived transactions.
  • Past students can only have archived transactions. All other students may have current transactions and/or archived transactions.
  • By following standard accounting practices, transactions can never be completely removed so that an audit trail exists.  Edits (adjustments in accounting terminology) that impact dollar amounts, will result in the addition of “A-“ before reference numbers (e.g. invoice number 1234 will change to A-1234 if the fee amount is adjusted).
  • The person who added a transaction is listed as the “Operator”.  If the transaction amount is edited, the operator is replaced with the last person who modified the transaction. Technical note: The audit trail (in the database) actually records the operator for both the original and the edited transactions.

 

 

Fees menus

Choose Fees from the main menu. These are the main areas; click the cards to read more about each feature.

 

Student fees

Choose Fees > Student fees to record and track student-based fees.

 

 

   

Other school funds

Choose Fees > Other school funds to track non-student based funds submitted to the school.

 

   

Reports

Choose Fees > Reports to run reports specifically available for fee information. Some of these reports are also found in the Student reports area from the main menu. Each report is presented in a card with a brief description of the report.

 

 

Setup

Choose Setup > Fees to access applications that are usually set up once and then are occasionally reviewed as needed.
