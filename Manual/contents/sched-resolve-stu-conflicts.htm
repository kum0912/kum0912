Scheduling next year > Resolve student conflicts > Students with conflicts

 

The Students with conflicts option provides a list of students who were unable to get into all the courses they requested. Some schools may opt to counsel students individually, and others may make changes to the timetable based on the student’s alternate course selections (e.g. if the option to substitute alternate courses for preferred courses was not used during the load students process).  

 

This option is used late in the scheduling process, specifically AFTER the school has completed these 2 processes for the very last time:

 

  • the classes are scheduled (the master timetable is complete) AND
  • the load students process has been run for the last time.

 

To see students with conflicts choose Scheduling next year > Resolve student conflicts.

 

 

Select GO on the Students with conflicts card. The students with conflicts option will list the students as shown here:

 

 

You will see the type of conflict for each student::

 

Subjects not yet assigned to periods (Courses not yet scheduled): The student has a preferred course selection, but that course has no classes scheduled.  This may appear when accessing this option before the school’s master timetable is completed. If the timetable is still in progress, do not take the time to resolve any student conflicts because running the load students process may undo all your changes. Use the Unscheduled classes option to see the list of courses that are not yet fully scheduled.  

 

Conflict (Multiple conflicts (no timetable)): The student was not able to get scheduled into 2 or more of their preferred course selections. The timetable has been left empty so that the school can determine which classes are best to include/exclude from the timetable. Select the student (row click), to open a new browser window showing the edit of the student’s next year’s timetable along with the courses that were not scheduled. It will be similar to the example above, however every course will appear as a course selection. Select a cell to schedule the student into a class section (see edit timetable for more info).

 

Partially loaded (Single conflict): The student was able to get scheduled into all but one of their preferred course selections.  Select the student (row click), to open a new browser window showing the edit of the student’s next year’s timetable along with the course that was not scheduled. Important concepts when resolving conflicts:

 

  • Timetables may be altered after the year end roll over (e.g. after transfer to new year process is completed). If you plan to resolve the student conflicts after the roll over, you may want to export the list of students by selecting the export icon in the tool bar.
