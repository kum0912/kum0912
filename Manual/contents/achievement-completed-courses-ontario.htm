Overview

This section contains information specific to Ontario schools. Be sure to also refer to the main completed courses section for additional information that applies to all schools, regardless of province.

 

Ontario secondary schools are responsible for generating a student’s official transcript, following all guidelines required for the establishment, maintenance, issue and storage of the Ontario Student Transcript (OST). The OST form is available in English and French.  Refer to the OST manual, published by the Ministry of Education, for regulations and procedures that apply to the recording of information in various situations. This manual is periodically revised and it may be downloaded from http://www.edu.gov.on.ca/eng/general/elemsec/ost/ost.html.

 

Since the1999-2000 school year, Ontario schools have been required to provide a complete record of students’ performance in grade 11 and 12 course, where both successful and unsuccessful attempts at completing grade 11 and 12 courses must appear on the OST.

 

The OST will include:

 

  • all Grade 9 and 10 courses successfully completed by the student, with percentage grades obtained and credits earned;  
  • all Grade 11 and 12 courses completed successfully or attempted unsuccessfully by the student, with percentage grades obtained and credits earned;  
  • all equivalent credits granted through the Prior Learning Assessment and Recognition (PLAR) equivalency process under OS/OSS or through the equivalency process under OSIS;  
  • all Grade 10 courses for which the student successfully challenged for credit through the PLAR challenge process, with percentage grades obtained and credits earned;  
  • all Grade 11 and 12 courses for which the student successfully or unsuccessfully challenged for credit through the PLAR challenge process, with percentage grades obtained and credits earned; • identification of compulsory credits, including credits that are substitutions for compulsory credits identified by the ministry as diploma requirements;  
  • confirmation that the student has completed the forty hours of community involvement;  
  • confirmation that the student has successfully completed the provincial secondary school literacy requirement.  

 

Since September 1, 1999, the OST has also had to include a record of the achievement of exceptional students who have taken “alternative courses”. Alternative courses are non-credit, individualized courses that are documented in a student’s Individual Education Plan (IEP) and that comprise alternative expectations – that is, expectations not found in the Ontario curriculum. These are also known as ‘K courses’.

 

The completed courses option in the student’s record provides a record of the student’s completed courses that may include courses that are not part of the official transcript (e.g. grade 9 and 10 courses attempted but not successfully completed).  The official OST generated for the student will automatically include only those courses which are required on the transcript.

 

Courses are automatically added to the student’s transcript when the school runs Achievement > Completed courses > Add completed courses.  

 

When a student drops a grade 11 or 12 course, a dialogue will appear so that the school can decide whether this withdrawn course should be added to the transcript along with their current percentage grade as per this OST requirement:  

 

For students who have withdrawn from a course after five instructional days following the issue of the first provincial report card in a semestered school or five instructional days following the issue of the second provincial report card in a non-semestered school, enter the student’s percentage grade at the time of withdrawal.

  

If the student’s graduation requirements indicate that the student is in a Specialist High Skills Major (SHSM) program, then you will have access to additional features related to the categorization of the SHSM compulsory credits. For example, the student’s list of completed courses will include a column for the SHSM category, and if editing a course, a SHSM tab is available to specify credit substitutions (just like the SUB./EQUIVALENCY CREDITS tab for regular graduation requirements except these are for SHSM requirements). The SHSM record may be printed in addition to the student’s transcript.

Due to the number of fields recorded for each completed course, it may be necessary to scroll to the right to see more fields, or select the row with the course (e.g. row click) to access the Details option to see all the fields for that one completed course.

 

The completed courses area also contains class attendance information that is automatically transferred based on the daily attendance taken by the school.  

 

Within edsembli SIS, schools can track the compulsory credits for graduation by categorizing each credit earned. If a completed course has multiple credits in different categories (e.g. a PLE, EQV or Q course in Ontario), or if the completed course is to be considered a substitution for a required credit, then the SUB./EQUIVALENCY CREDIT tab may be used for this purpose. Ontario schools have a SHSM tab option as well for substitutions/equivalency credits related to a student’s SHSM program (it functions the same except that it shows the student’s SHSM requirements rather than the regular graduation requirements).  For more information on this function, see the main Completed courses documentation.

   

Completed courses fields (Ontario)

Adding or editing a completed course will provide access to the following fields on the COURSE tab:

 

Course code, title and grade:  Indicates the course the student completed. The title, grade and course number (if applicable) are all attributes of the course and are automatically set based on the setup of the code). For those unfamiliar with Ontario course codes, the Ministry’s OST documentation provides a brief explanation of the course codes.  

 

Note about the printed transcript:  

 

  • The course grade will appear as N/A for alternative courses which do not lead to credit (courses that start with K). These courses should be flagged automatically as “Alternative expectations”  and ‘A’ will show in the Notes column.
  • Courses that start with FSF, FEF or FIF will show ‘F’ in the Notes column.

 

Mark:  The student’s final mark in the course. Any alphanumeric mark is allowed up to 3 characters, with a maximum numeric mark of 100. Equivalent credit courses (courses that start with EQV, PLE or Q) have no mark so EQV will appear automatically. Alternative courses which do not lead to credits (aka K courses) may have a numeric mark entered or the letter L if ‘ALT’ is to appear as the student’s mark.  

 

Credits attempted:  Indicates the credits of the course that would be applied to the student if successfully completed the course. This value is based on the credits of the class that was in the student’s timetable (if automatically added) or based on the setup of the code (if manually added). The attempted credits may be edited, if needed, to any number up to 99.99.   

 

Note about the printed transcript:

 

  • The credit will always format automatically to 2 decimals.

 

Credits earned: Indicates the credits that the student has earned. Earned credits will be automatically set to the same value as the attempted credits when a mark of 50 or higher is entered. This may be changed, if needed. Withdrawn courses, as required by OST requirements, will have W for the credit value.  When a student has a passing mark, and a credit value of 0, the credit will appear as R (for repeat) on the list of completed courses and on the printed transcript. Repeated grade 11 and 12 courses are included on the official OST.

 

Notes about the printed transcript:

 

  • the credits will appear as N/A for alternative courses which do not lead to credit (courses that start with K).  
  • the word “partial” will appear after the course title if the student earns fewer credits that the attempted credits and the course is a multiple-credit college or university delivered dual credit course for which the passing grade established by the college or university is greater than 50% (e.g. the student received partial credits for the parts of the course that the student successfully completed).  

 

Completion date: The date on which the student completed the requirements for a course, or the date the student withdrew from a course (applicable to grade 11 or 12 courses only).

 

Note about the printed transcript

 

  • Only the year and month appear on the printed transcript in the format yyyy/mm.

 

School:  Indicates the school where the course was taken. The board is responsible for the setup of the schools that are available for selection in this field. Start typing the school name then select the school from the list of matching schools.

 

Note about the printed transcript:

 

  • If the school is not this school, the printed transcript can optionally include an asterisk before the date to identify courses completed at other schools.
  • If the school is flagged as a private school in the school setup configuration, a “P” will appear in the Notes column when the transcript is printed from a publicly funded secondary school (P = credit earned at an inspected private school by a student registered in a publicly funded school)

 

Category: Displays the category of the course. This will be used to automatically determine whether the earned credits will be considered compulsory credits (e.g. credits that the student is required to fulfil as part of graduation requirements).   

 

If a completed course has multiple credits in different categories (e.g. a PLE, EQV or Q course in Ontario), or if the completed course is to be considered a substitution for a required credit, then the SUB./EQUIVALENCY CREDIT tab may be used for this purpose. Ontario schools have a SHSM tab option as well for substitutions/equivalency credits related to a student’s SHSM program (it functions the same except that it shows the student’s SHSM requirements rather than the regular graduation requirements).  For more information on this function, see the main Completed courses documentation.

 

Notes about the printed transcript:

 

  • The compulsory column in the transcript is automatically determined.
  • A “X” will appear in this column for courses in which a compulsory credit has been earned.
  • For partial compulsory credits (e.g., Civics, Grade 10), the earned credit value will appear for the compulsory course rather than an X (e.g. 0.50).  
  • When one credit of a two-credit cooperative education course is being used as a compulsory credit, a ‘1’ appears in this column. This is automatically determined.
  • When the category is changed from the default, it will be considered a substitution and will generate an X in the “Note” column. Such substitutions can only be made with the approval of the principal.  
  • H will appear in the notes column if the course is a compulsory credit for the student’s SHSM program. This is automatically determined.

 

Course attendance – Absent, Excused, Late and Possible:  Attendance info is automatically recorded when the course is automatically added, if the class is taking regular period attendance (rather than am/pm attendance).  This info includes the number of times the student was absent or excused from class, or late to class.  The ‘Possible’ field represents the number of times the class was taught and the student was expected to attend.

 

Prior Learning Assessment and Recognition (PLAR) - PLAR type, Mature credit:  Regular day school students who transfer to an Ontario secondary school from home schooling in Ontario, from a non-inspected private school in Ontario, or from a school outside Ontario may be granted equivalent credits through the PLAR equivalency process for regular day school students based on the principal’s evaluation of their previous learning. Such credits may be granted through the equivalency process or the challenge process. For these courses, the PLAR type needs to be set to either Equivalency (Q, PLE and EQV courses) or Challenge.

 

Mature credit is an optional yes/no field to indicate whether the principal used the PLAR process for mature students.

 

Course details – Instructional language, Delivery, Course type, Dual credit: These fields are automatically assigned values based on the class that was in the student’s timetable (if automatically added) or based on the setup of the code (if manually added). They may be changed if needed. The Dual credit field appears if the course type is set to dual credits.  

 

Notes about the printed transcript:

 

  • If the course type is set to a co-op value, ‘C’ will appear in the notes column.
  • If the dual credit is set to a team-taught value, ‘T’ will appear in the notes column.
  • If the instructional language is set to French in an English school, and the category is not Anglais, French or Français, then ‘F’ will appear in the notes column. ‘F’ also appears for courses starting with FSF, FIF, FEF.

 

Include in GPA calculations: Yes/no field to indicate whether this course is to be included in any GPA (grade point average) calculations that the school may want to use for their own purposes.

 

OST notes column – Special indicator, Modified curriculum, Alternative expectation, Interdisciplinary studies: Check these yes/no fields if applicable to the student’s completed course.  If special indicator = yes, ‘S’ will appear in the notes column; if modified curriculum = yes, ‘M’ will appear; if Alternative expectation = yes, ‘A’ will appear; if Interdisciplinary studies = yes, I will appear.

 

Notes about the printed transcript:  The following notations may appear in this column:  

 

  • S = special indicator noting that the student’s performance in a Grade 11 or 12 course or an OAC was affected by extraordinary circumstances
  • I = interdisciplinary studies  
  • F = course taught in French in a Core, Extended, or Immersion program  
  • C = cooperative education  
  • X = substitution for a compulsory credit course  
  • H = a required Specialist High Skills Major course  
  • T = a team-taught dual credit course  
  • P = credit earned at an inspected private school by a student registered in a publicly funded school  
  • M = modified curriculum expectations that do not lead to a credit  
  • A = alternative expectations, which do not lead to a credit

Settings

The Transcript button will be defaulted; select the SHSM record button if that needs to be printed instead.

 

For the transcript, you may print an Official and Unofficial transcript.

 

Official transcript will automatically include only those courses which are required on the transcript.

  • Indicate with asterisk previous school courses. On both the official and unofficial transcript, courses completed at another school may be indicated with an asterisk
  • Course code length (e.g. 5 to 7 characters of the course code).  

 

Unofficial transcript has additional options:

  • Include grade 9/10 withdrawn courses,
  • Include grade 9/10 failed courses,
  • Include zero credit courses (e.g. courses that are not part of the Ministry’s official common course codes),
  • Include requirements legend (showing the student’s graduation requirements).
  • Indicate with asterisk previous school courses. On both the official and unofficial transcript, courses completed at another school may be indicated with an asterisk.
  • Course code length (e.g. 5 to 7 characters of the course code).
