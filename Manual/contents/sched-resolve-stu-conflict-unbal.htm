Scheduling next year > Resolve student conflicts > Unbalanced timetables

After the you have assigned students to classes (student timetables are built), and have run the final load students, you may want to identify students with unbalanced timetables with respect to the expected course work load. Each course can have a weighting value to represent the workload involved for the course material.  The weighting value can be any number from 1 to 10.  A weighting of 1 is defaulted for all courses.

 

This weighting is used to determine whether a student’s timetable is balanced between semesters or terms.  By summing the course weights of each class in the student’s timetable for the semester or term, if the weight totals are different, the student has an unbalanced timetable.

 

Choose Scheduling next year > Resolve student conflicts.

 

Select Go on the Unbalanced timetables card. This option will default to show the sum of the course weightings by semester as shown here:

 

Use the option buttons to switch between the 3 view options; By semester, By term, and Show all, as explained below.

 

By semester:  identifies students with unbalanced course workloads between semesters, as shown in the example above.  If the school has more than 1 term, the semester weighting will be the average of the term weightings.
