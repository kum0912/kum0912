Overview

Most schools and board/division have other applications that track detailed staff and payroll information. edsembli’s staff records require minimal information for staff members that are involved in the students’ education, as well as those staff members that require access to edsembli Connect. Additional staff may be optionally added.  

At a minimum, each staff record requires the person’s name. Boards/divisions should provide guidance on what information must be recorded for each staff record. For example, some provinces collect information on a teacher’s classes, requiring the entry of the provincial teacher number. The staff member’s board/division email address is also often required if security access to edsembli 1.0 is provided through an authenticated service.

Each staff member is considered a single person with a single staff record, however that person may be a staff member at multiple schools. Staff also exist at the board/division (central office). When a new staff member is added, if the staff member matches another staff member already in the board/division, they will be considered the same staff member. See Add staff for more information on this process.

When a staff member is active in more than 1 school, the person specific data may be edited by either school. If this happens, the following message will appear “Edits applied to other schools. If appropriate, notify other schools”.

The following staff information is considered person specific:

Name and numbers

All other information is considered school specific, meaning that the information is unique for each school.

Each school is responsible for assigning staff members (teachers) to homerooms, advisor groups, and classes, and this information is available for viewing from the staff record, however, it cannot be edited here.

Class information defined by the school is used to create the teacher’s timetable.

 

Search staff

To access a staff member’s record, select the Menu icon in the left corner, then select Staff records then Search staff from the menu.

 

 

From the resulting screen (shown below), enter as much or as little of the staff member’s name as you want then select the search button or Enter key. For example, enter ‘B’ for a list of staff whose surnames start with B, or enter ‘Babcock’ for a list of staff whose surnames start with Babcock. If you know the staff member’s usual name (e.g. Joshua’s usual name is Joe), enter ‘Babcock, Joe’ to go directly to the staff record. Your search may show multiple staff matching the search name, so make sure you select the right one. Hint: enter * for a list of all staff.

 

 

Available filters include:

Only teachers: check box option

  • Surname, Given name
  • Given name

  • Role

Considered staff registered:

  • This and next year
  • This year only (not returning)

Menu

The staff record represents a single access point for the information recorded for the staff member. The information is broken down into areas based on the type of information recorded for the person. Using the menu on the left, the content (shown as the Name area above) will change to reflect the menu option selected.

 

 

The staff records menu can be hidden or shown by selecting the icon in the top right corner of the staff member’s profile. The same icon is now used for show vs hide, however the tool tip will correctly indicate whether selecting the icon will hide the menu, or show the menu. Note: this icon will appear in the toolbar on smaller devices.

 

Use this icon to show or hide the staff record menu and profile area.

 

 

Staff records has an advanced search feature that lets you search for a specific area in the record (e.g. name). Enter criteria beside the Search icon and results will automatically display.

 

 

For further information on menus, see Menus in Common Features.

 

App bar

The App bar for Staff records appears across the top of the staff member’s record. Use the Menu icon in the top left corner to navigate to other areas of Maplewood connectEd (e.g. leave the staff records area).

 

 

There are some convenient icons in the top right corner:

 

The Select staff icon calls up the Select staff screen.

 

 

The PREVIOUS and NEXT icons move (step) to the next or previous staff in the list of resulting staff (e.g. the staff matching the search criteria used to find this person).

  

 

The New window icon lets you keep your existing browser window (tab) open while opening a new browser tab with 3 possible options. The options will appear when the icon is selected as shown here:

  

  

 

New staff: takes you to the select staff interface where a new staff member may be selected

edsembli dashboard: takes you to the initial Maplewood connectEd dashboard page

Duplicate staff: creates another browser session with the same staff information (creates a duplicate of the page you were looking at).

 

This is handy because you can have multiple staff records open at the same time.

  

Hint: browser tabs can be maximized or restored down to smaller sizes so that they can be repositioned side by side.

 

Profile

The staff Profile area contains the staff’s picture, and other pertinent information for easy reference. Pictures are usually imported annually, but if a staff member’s picture is not available or needs updating, click the staff picture area to select an image from your source.

 

 

Hint: to quickly see the staff photo in full saved size right click a staff’s picture, then choose Open image in a new tab. This works only in Chrome browser.

 

The profile area shows the staff member’s usual names and legal names. Usual names appear first and are formatted as Usual Name and Surname. Legal names are displayed second, in a smaller font, and are formatted as legal surname, given name + middle name(s) + , suffix (if applicable).

 

 

Status is a quick way to organize staff based on when they are/have or will be working at this school. Every staff is assigned a status reflective of the staff member’s employment at the school being accessed. The following statuses are available:

 

This year and next year: staff member is actively working at the school this year and is scheduled to work at the school next year. *

This year only: staff member is actively working at the school this year but is not expected to be working at this school next year. *

Next year only: staff member is working at this school next year (e.g. new to the school next year). Next year only staff members show their next year homeroom and advisor group in the profile area (if they are a homeroom or advisor group teacher).

Inactive: Staff was working at this school at some point this school year but has since left this school.

Past: Staff worked at the school in previous years.

 

The following additional information will appear in the profile area, if data exists for the staff member:

 

Employee no.: Local employee number for board/division identification.

Provincial teaching number: (field name changes depending on your province e.g. Teacher certificate # for Alberta). Field is only applicable if the staff member is an educator (teacher).

Homeroom: If applicable, the staff member’s current homeroom designation and room number.

Advisor group: If applicable, the staff member’s current advisor group name and room number. Advisor groups are similar to homerooms where a room and teacher(s) is/are assigned to the advisor group. The name of the group can be anything the school wants. Some schools use advisor groups to indicate the guidance councillor assigned to the student. Some schools have students meet regularly in groups where the students meet with an advisory teacher to discuss general school related topics.

The DETAILS button located in the lower right corner of the card will take you actual location of the card in the staff menu, where Edit functionality would be available if appropriate.

 

Personal

Name

The Name card contains the staff member’s preferred (usual) names as well as the exact legal names as provided on an official source document. For most staff, the surname field is the same as their legal surname.  

 

 

As the staff member’s name is edited, previously recorded names are maintained in the Name changes area for reference only (and therefore may not be edited). Name change information is collapsed by default on the card, but may be expanded if you want to see the person’s previous names. When searching for staff, the search may optionally consider matches based on the information in the name changes area.

 

There is a Y/N switch to indicate whether the legal surname is the same as the surname. When the switch is set to Yes, legal surname is not available.

If the switch is set to No, legal surname fields is available.

 

The following fields are recorded as part of the staff name:

 

Surname: The person’s preferred surname.

Legal surname same as surname (Y/N flag): If no, legal surname field is available.

Legal surname: If flag above is N, this field is enabled for the entry of the legal surname. If the flag above is Y, this field does not appear.

Suffix: If applicable, the suffix appears after the surname such as Jr., Sr., III).

Given name: The person’s first name (typically as it would appear on a legal document).

Middle name(s): The person’s middle name or names.

Usual name: The person’s preferred first name or names. It will default the given name.

Demographics

The demographics card, shown below, contains the following fields:

Birthdate: Person’s date of birth. This information is typically optional, however Saskatchewan provincial reporting does require a birthdate for K to 9 teachers as part of their educator class assignment data collection.

Gender: Person’s gender (select from the list).

 

 

Contact info

The staff member’s board/division email address is required if security access to edsembli 1.0 is provided through an authenticated service.  

The Email card contains the person’s email information. There is no limit to the number of email addresses that may be recorded. Each email is given an order number, so that the number that is ordered as 1 will appear first on the card.

 

 

The following fields are available for each email:

 

Email: Email address (e.g. yourname@school.ca)

Order: The order in which the email should appear in the list when multiple emails exist.

Type: Select a description type from the drop down list (e.g. school, personal). Duplicates are permitted (e.g. student may have 2 school emails).

Note: optional note that may be recorded pertaining to this specific email.

Availability: Switch between Yes or No for the following attributes that may or may not apply to this email:

  • Day (is the email address available during the day)
  • Night (is the email address available during the night)

 

The Contact details card is for the tracking of the person’s consent to receive unsolicited electronic communication. This tracking is required as part of the Canadian anti-spam legislation (CASL).

Each time consent information is changed, a new record is added to track the consent type (Expressed yes, expressed no, implied consent, or unknown), along with a note (e.g. to describe how you were informed of the consent type). Each record is automatically assigned today’s date. Only one entry per date is allowed and therefore adding two records on the same date will only save the most recent record. For audit purposes, consent information recorded on previous dates may not be edited or removed.

When a person expresses no to receiving unsolicited electronic communication, an anti-spam icon will appear on that person’s email card.

 

Class info

The Class info menu option will only appear if the staff member is assigned to at least one class, homeroom, or advisor group in this school, considering class info for this school year or next school year. Therefore, the class info area will not appear for most non-teaching staff, unless they are assigned to an advisor group.

 

Classes

Homeroom and advisor group information is displayed in the Classes sub-menu. The Timetable sub-menu is where the teacher’s class schedule information is displayed.

Edits to the teacher’s class information is not possible from the staff member’s record. The staff record is presenting the information set up by the school.

 

 

The Classes sub-menu contains the teacher’s current location and homeroom/advisor group info cards, if applicable.

 

  • Current location

If the staff member’s timetable contains at least one class in a period today, the Current location card is available to indicate where the teacher may be located right now by showing the class, room and teacher information for the current period (based on the current time compared to the school’s period times). If the teacher is not assigned a class in the current period, the words “Teacher is not in any class at this time” will appear. Use the period tab strip to change to a different period.

If the staff member is teaching a class at another school, the background will be shaded.

 

  • Homeroom/advisor group info

If the staff member is assigned to a homeroom or an advisor group for the current year, this card will appear.  Homeroom information includes the homeroom designation and room number, along with the name(s) of the staff member(s) assigned to the homeroom. Similarly, advisor group information includes the group name and room number, along with the name(s) of the staff member(s) assigned to the advisor group.

If the teacher is assigned to a homeroom or advisor group in another school in the board/division, you'll see a card titled Homeroom/advisor group info – Other schools.

 

Timetable

The Timetable option presents the teacher’s classes organized by periods, days, terms and semesters. Each school’s setup defines the number of semesters, terms in a semester, days in cycle, and periods per day. Each class offered by the school is assigned to the appropriate semester(s), term(s), day(s) and period(s) that correspond to where that class is taught. This information is used to determine where the class appears in the teacher’s timetable. If all classes in a teacher’s timetable are full year (scheduled for all semesters), or if the school’s setup is 1 semester, any references to semesters will be hidden. If all classes in a teacher’s timetable are scheduled for all terms, or if the school’s setup is 1 term, any references to terms will be hidden. If the teacher’s classes do not rotate between days, all days will be combined rather than showing a timetable for day 1 and another timetable for day 2, etc.

Employment

Staff employment

The Employment card contains the staff member’s Maplewood status and a Y/N switch to indicate whether or not the person is a teacher. Use the Edit icon to change this information, if necessary.

Maplewood status is a quick way to organize staff based on when they are/have been/will be working at this school. Every staff is assigned a status reflective of the staff member’s employment at the school being accessed.

 

 

 

The following statuses are available:

 

This year and next year: staff member is actively working at the school this year and is scheduled to work at the school next year. *

This year only: staff member is actively working at the school this year but is not expected to be working at this school next year. *

Next year only: staff member is working at this school next year (e.g. new to the school next year). Next year only staff members show their next year homeroom and advisor group in the profile area (if they are a homeroom or advisor group teacher).

Withdrawn or Inactive: Staff member was working at this school at some point this school year but has since left this school.

Past: Staff member worked at the school in previous years.

 

Teacher: If the staff member is to be assigned to any class or homeroom, the teacher switch should be enabled (set to yes). If it is set to no, the staff member will not be available for assignment (their name will not appear in the class and homeroom teacher drop down lists).

 

Note: it may be necessary to remove the teacher from classes, homerooms, or advisor groups before changing their Maplewood status or setting the Teacher switch to no. If a teacher is leaving the school part way through the school year, first assign a new teacher to the classes, homerooms, and advisor groups, and then edit the employment information in the staff record.
