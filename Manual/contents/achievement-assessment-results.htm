Student records > Achievement > Assessment results

Many assessment tests (also referred to as Standardized Tests) are available to schools in order to assess students for the purpose of improving student learning. Here are a few example assessment tests commonly used in Canada: Reading Recovery, Northern Lights, ORR, Fountas & Pinnell, DRA, PM Benchmark, Number Sense, GB+, Alpha-Jeunes, and EQAO (in Ontario only). 

The results of these assessments may be recorded for each student involved in the tests and they are retained as part of the student's record for historical reference. As the students are evaluated on an assessment/test, the results of the test may be entered (typically by the teacher using the school wide assessments feature in edsembli CONNECT. Results may also be imported if the results are available in a file (e.g. when results are determined outside of the school as in Ontario's EQAO tests).   

From the student's record, assessment results will appear for only those assessments that have info previously entered for the student. The board/division/district has to set up the assessment and then the teacher must have made an entry for the student in the assessment, otherwise the assessment will not appear.

Assessment results are visible by choosing the Assessment results option under the Achievement sub-menu.

 

Each assessment appears in a unique card corresponding to the name of the assessment (e.g. DRA Reading assessment). The subjects/strands set up for the assessment appear in the first column, and then the 'assessment periods' appear in the remaining columns. Only those periods where the student has results will appear.

Users with access to edit this information, may change the results by selecting the Edit icon, but they cannot add any new assessments. Only the teachers can do this.
