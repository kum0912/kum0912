Overview

This option is for Ontario schools only.

 

A Ministry of Education requirement, the French Card is an individual record of accumulated instruction in French as a second language. This card must be maintained for each student in an elementary school and stored within the Ontario Student Record (OSR). An entry must be made on the record:

 

  • at the end of each school year, semester or summer school course
  • when a student transfers to another school, including private, federal or First Nations School
  • when the student retires form the school.

 

The French/Indigenous hours option in a student’s record provides an annual summary of the student's accumulated hours of instruction in French or an Indigenous language. The hours are automatically recorded as part of the year-end roll over process (transfer to current), allowing the school to simply 'Print' a report for French or Indigenous language accumulated hours. Information may be optionally added manually at any time.

 

This information is available for current, past and future students, and is student-specific, which means the information is the same regardless of the school that the student attends. Only the school that is the edsembli record owner can edit the student’s promotion history.

 

The French/Indigenous hours option is located in the Previous years submenu.

 

 

Once selected, the Accumulated instruction in French/Indigenous languages card, becomes available.

 

Accumulated instruction in French/Indigenous languages

The Accumulated instruction in French/Indigenous languages card will list the student’s hours in French and Indigenous languages. This info is updated annually at the end of the school year. 

 

 

Use the Add icon in the tool bar for a new entry.

 

  

 

Date of completion: This date typically represents the last school day of the school year. If the student left the school mid-year, then the student’s exit date should be used.

 

Grade: This is the student’s current grade at the end of the school year, (or exit date), and represents the grade that the student was in during that particular school year.

 

Subject: If the instruction hours was based on the enrolment in a specific course, optionally select the course.

 

School name: The school where the hours were completed. This will be automatically set to the school who added the record (e.g. this school when adding a new record) and it may not be edited.

 

Exemption: Yes/No field that may be set to yes if the schools wants to track that the student was exempt for a year (and therefore no hours were recorded).

 

Board #: The board number where the hours of instruction were taken. This is a required field and will default this board # when the record is added. This may be edited, if needed.

 

French hours of instruction: The number of hours of instruction in French the student received in that grade for that year.

 

Indigenous hours of instruction: The number of hours of instruction in an Indigenous language the student received in that grade for that year.

 

Once all the required fields are populated, the entry may be saved by clicking SAVE.

 

To edit an entry, click an entry to access the Edit option. When editing an entry, the delete icon will appear in the tool bar.

 

 

Printing the French or Indigenous language card

Use the Print icon to access the print functionality. When selected, the icon will present 2 additional icons corresponding to the French language (FL) or Indigenous language (IL) report (aka French card and Indigenous language card).
