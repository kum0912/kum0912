Intro for school office staff

 

The school office staff includes all individuals working in the school's main office, school administrators, guidance and resource staff. Each individual has specific responsibilities that are essential to the operation of the school and edsembli SIS provides the applications you need to perform your job efficiently and effectively.

 

By doing our job well, we ensure that you can maintain accurate student information. Pertinent student information is shared with many educational stakeholders, from the government agencies for funding purposes, the board/division/district supervisors, the teachers, the parents/guardians and the students. Depending upon your province and board/division/district, the data may also be shared with other agencies that have permission to this data such as colleges and universities, health units, learning management systems, career counseling applications, data warehouses, to name just a few possible examples. Without a doubt, all school office staff are key participants in the maintenance of accurate student data and are the vital link between the student and all education stakeholders.

 

For most schools, by the time you start using edsembli SIS, most of the setup has been done as part of the implementation, and the student data has been added. Based on this assumption, we would suggest you first start with the Student Records portion of the manual. Each student in the database has a student record which provides easy access to the wealth of data recorded for the student.

 

Once you have an understanding of the type of data that is available, read the sections of the manual that pertain to the functions used to build that data (such as attendance, achievement, and scheduling). Become familiar with the reports that are available, particularly if you are responsible for provincial, federal, and other agency reporting. There are some setup areas that you need to be familiar with, particularly if you are responsible for maintaining the master list of homerooms or classes in the school.

 

If you are a school administrator with teaching duties as well, please make sure you also read the section called Introduction for teachers.

 

We also strongly suggest that all users of edsembli SIS, read the section titled Common Features which explains features of the application that are the same regardless of your role and access rights.

 

For most boards/divisions/districts, by the time you start using edsembli SIS, most of the setup has been done as part of the implementation, and the school and student data has been added. Based on this assumption, we would suggest you first start with the Student Records portion of the manual. Each student in the database has a student record which provides easy access to the wealth of data recorded for the student. Typically, central office staff have view rights to student data only, with all edits performed by those with permissions in the schools.
