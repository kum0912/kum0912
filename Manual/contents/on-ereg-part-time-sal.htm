Enrolment registers - Part-time SAL

 

Supervised Alternative Learning (SAL) may be useful for encouraging some young people of compulsory school age to continue their learning, if they are at risk of not graduating because they are not attending school regularly and if other retention strategies have not been successful. Students with SAL plans are reported on regular full-time/part-time registers.

 

When a student enrolls in a SAL program, the student’s attendance type field must be set to SAL. Then the student’s SAL attendance type will determine whether the student appears on the full-time register, or if they appear on this part-time SAL register. The Ministry has approved this part-time SAL register as a way for the school to separate these students from the non-SAL part-time students.   

 

When a student is enrolled in SAL, follow these steps:

 

  • In the student’s record, go to Enrolment > Enrolment register info. Set the attendance type field to SAL.
  • In the student’s record, go to Enrolment > Supervised Alternative Learning (SAL). Set the student’s SAL attendance type to one of the following:  

 

- Less than 70 minutes of day school instruction

- More than or equal to 70 minutes of day school instruction

 

Based on the SAL attendance type value given to the student, the SAL student will appear on one of the official registers as follows:

 

Full-time register, with an FTE of 1.0, if the pupil’s SAL plan includes an average of at least 70 minutes of instruction per school day in which the pupil may earn a credit.

  

Part-time, with an FTE of 0.50, if the pupil’s SAL plan includes an average of less than 70 minutes of instruction per school day in which the pupil may earn a credit.  

 

To access the part-time SAL register, select Ontario reporting > Enrolment registers > Part-time SAL.

 

 

See the detailed instructions for the Part-time card for clarification on the options/reports generated from this card.  Part-time SAL is identical to Part-time, except that it only includes the students who are part-time SAL as described earlier.
