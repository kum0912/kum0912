Student reports > Federal > Nominal role & Special education

 

Overview

Indigenous and Northern Affairs Canada (INAC) has an annual Nominal Roll Student Census Report and a Special Education Program Report. Please note that INAC was formerly Aboriginal Affairs and Northern Development Canada (INAC).

 

This feature can generate files to assist with this federal reporting to INAC. By creating XML files in the format required by INAC, these files may be submitted to their School Information System Export (SISE) application. A file containing nominal role information may be created from individual schools or centrally from the board/division. A file containing special education expenses may be created from individual schools only.

 

INAC's Nominal Roll Report is used to create an information database for regions, districts and schools of eligible students living on reserve or on Crown land, in receipt of any education assistance from INAC. The students in the census must be attending a school that meets the elementary/secondary curriculum standards and fiscal framework of the Ministry of Education for the province/territory of residence.

 

INAC’s Special Education Program Report identifies high cost special education expenses for students who are eligible for INAC funding.

 

The following requirements must be met in order for a student to be considered Eligible and therefore included in XML files created by Maplewood.

 

  • Student is enrolled in the school on September 30th of the current school year. The student’s entry date(s) and exit/retirement date(s) are used to determine whether the student is enrolled in the school on the count date.
  • Student’s birthdate indicates that the student will be at least four years of age and less than 21 years of age by December 31 of the current school year.
  • Each province has a different field that must be set correctly to indicate that the student is an eligible first nations student.

 

British Columbia, Manitoba: The student’s Citizenship must be set to Native Ancestry

 

Alberta: The student’s Enrolment type must be set to the value 330 (Indian student for whom the Education School Authority receives payment of a tuition from the Government of Canada). Enrolment type is located on the Enrolment Reporting card available from the Enrolment, Student Enrolment menu of the student’s record.

 

Saskatchewan: The student’s Aboriginal Status code is set to Inuit, Métis and/or treaty. Aboriginal status code is located on the Aboriginal card available from the Personal, Aboriginal menu of the student’s record.

 

Ontario: The student’s Student Board Resident Status field must be set to code 02 (Native Education Authority.

 

Prior to creating any files, please check that the above information is correctly specified for any students who are to be included in these reports. All fields in these files are noted in the charts following this documentation. Please review these charts to understand what information is included in the file. In particular, review the Nominal Role fields to ensure that the data is correctly set up.

 

  • To create a Nominal role report, choose Student reports > Enrolment and select RUN on the Nominal roll card.
  • To create a Special education report, choose Student reports > Enrolment and select RUN on the Special education card.

Contact info

The CONTACT INFO tab contains contact information in the event that INAC needs additional information about the report. There are 3 contacts, each with their own cards: Certifier (mandatory), Primary contact (mandatory), and Secondary contact (optional). If the certifier is also either the primary or secondary contact, select Y to the ‘Same as certifier’ field on the cards to avoid re-entering data.

 

 

For each contact, enter the family name (i.e. surname), given name, job title, email address, and phone number (area code/phone/extension).

 

Use the Search icon if the contact exists in the staff records. Once you select the staff member the information will be automatically copied to these INAC contact fields.

 

Note: this information will not be automatically updated if the staff record is subsequently changed.  

 

 

Special education

The Special Education Program Report is designed to help eligible students identified with high-cost special needs to access quality programs and services that are culturally sensitive and reflective of generally accepted provincial/territorial standards. INAC's Special Education Program consists of two major components.

 

Direct Services – these classroom and school-based services focus directly on meeting the First Nation student’s needs, such as student assessments and professional services (e.g. education psychologists, speech and language therapists, counsellors) for the student. At least 75% of AANDC's special education funding is used for direct services.

 

Indirect Services – these services are provided to eligible First Nation recipients for the development of special education programs and services such as professional development for teachers, para-professionals and parents and research and development of special education approaches and programs.

 

Services provided under the Special Education Program are based on the understanding that each First Nation student with special education needs is unique and requires a customized program to meet their individual needs in order to best support their success.

Contact info

The CONTACT INFO tab contains contact information in the event that INAC needs additional information about the report. There are 3 contacts, each with their own cards: Certifier (mandatory), Primary contact (mandatory), and Secondary contact (optional). If the certifier is also either the primary or secondary contact, select Y to the ‘Same as certifier’ field on the cards to avoid re-entering data.

Creating the report

Before creating the Nominal role report, please review the section titled Nominal role fields to ensure that you understand what data is used in this file. Similarly, before creating the Special education report, please review the section titled Special education fields.

 

To create the report (e.g. create the XML file), select the Create icon that you will see in the bottom right corner.

 

The XML file generated can uploaded to the INAC portal (School Information System Export (SISE) application), or it may be imported into an existing PDF where the information may be edited, if necessary.

Running this report centrally

The nominal role report can be run for multiple schools. When this report is run centrally (from the board/division), the Selected schools card (shown below) will be available.

 

Each of the selected schools be included in the report. Please check/edit the INAC# for each of the schools in the report.

 

Note: the special education report can’t be generated centrally.

    Term

    Definition

    Students with High Cost Special Education Needs

    Students eligible for funding under the Special Education Program (SEP) are those whose special education needs cannot be met within the resources intended for the general student population and who are on the Nominal Roll or eligible to be on the Nominal Roll based on the First Nation Education Program Guidelines.

    Broadly speaking, special education needs of students fall within a continuum of mild to moderate, moderate to severe and severe to profound. Special education needs that are at the lower end of this continuum are expected to be addressed within the resources intended for the general student population. Only special education needs that are more costly to address (generally ranging from moderate to profound) are eligible for funding under the SEP. Students eligible to receive programs and services under the Special Education Program (SEP) are to be identified as High Cost Special Education Students on the Nominal Roll.
