Student records > Enrolment > Supervised Alternative Learning (SAL)


Overview

This option is only available to students in grade 7 and higher attending Ontario publicly funded schools. 

 

http://www.edu.gov.on.ca/eng/document/forms/enrol/enrolment_register_instructions.pdf states

 

“Supervised Alternative Learning (SAL) may be useful for encouraging some young people of compulsory school age to continue their learning, if they are at risk of not graduating because they are not attending school regularly and if other retention strategies have not been successful. A pupil must be of between the ages of 14 and 17 to be in a SAL program. The one exception is the following: If any pupils begin a SAL program during the year when they are still of compulsory school age and then turn 18 between January 1 and June 30, they may choose to continue their SAL program until the end of that school year, as shown in the chart below. Subsection 21(1) of the Education Act states that children aged 6–17 must attend school, unless excused. Pupils must continue to attend school until they reach the age of 18 or until they graduate.”

 

When a student is placed into a SAL program, the student’s attendance type should be set to SAL for the duration of time when the student is enrolled in the SAL program. This is needed so that the student’s SAL information is included in the OnSIS batch files. The attendance type field is edited using the option Enrolment > Enrolment Register info in the student's record.  

 

Some schools/boards like to flag students in SAL for easy identification. See flags for more info.

 

Student’s in SAL require detailed information about their program (plan) so that it may be reported to the Ministry of Education as part of the OnSIS reporting.  This information is entered using the Supervised Alternative Learning (SAL) submenu option in the Enrolment menu of the student’s record.

 

 

 

Supervised Alternative Learning (SAL)

When this option is selected, and the student has no SAL info to show, a reminder will appear to set the student’s attendance type to SAL (using the Enrolment > Enrolment register info option in the student’s record).   

 

 

To add SAL info, select the ADD button in the lower right corner.  SAL info is recorded on an annual basis, so it will default to the current school year and there are some required fields that must be entered as explained below.   

 

Once SAL info is added, a SAL card will appear in the student’s record for each school year showing the info entered for the student, along with an Edit icon if anything requires changes. Additional school years may be added using the ADD button.

 

 

The following information may be recorded for each year the student is enrolled in a SAL program:

 

School year:  The school year (e.g. 2018-2019) must be selected during the initial add process when creating a new SAL record.  Once it is added, the school year appears in the title of the card. Only one card is available per school year.

 

SAL attendance type:  (required field) Select a SAL attendance type from the values:

  • Less than 70 minutes of day school instruction
  • More than or equal to 70 minutes of day school instruction

 

Note: Changing the student’s SAL attendance type will create an internal transfer.

 

SAL status type:  (required field) Select a SAL status type from the values:

  • First year in SAL
  • Previously participated in SAL

 

Start date:  (required field) Record the date the student started the SAL program.

 

End date:  Record the date the student ended or will end the SAL program this school year. For students returning to SAL next school year, it is recommended that the date of July 1 be used. OnSIS requires an end date at the end of each school year.

 

Exit type:  Select an Exit type from the values:

  • Graduated with an OSSD
  • Left school (on board list)
  • Left school (over compulsory school age)
  • Other
  • Returned to school full-time
  • Returned to school part-time
  • Returning to SAL next school year

 

OnSIS collects this field, and an exit type is required at the end of each school year.

 

Placement info:  Placement info is optional and may be used for the board’s own purposes.  Select the school staff member who will be the primary contact for anyone needing info about the student’s SAL program. Record the accumulated hours and minutes the student was in SAL, and the number of credits applied (which may be used to track credits working toward or credits earned or any other manner that the school would like to use).  The number of carry forward hours and minutes may also be recorded.

 

 

Plan components:  OnSIS collects the type(s) of components in the student’s SAL plan. At least one plan component must exist for the student. Use the Add icon in the Plan components card to add a new component, otherwise select an existing component (row click) to edit the component info.

 

 

For each component, the following fields are available:

 

Plan component: (required field) Select a plan component from the values:

  • Continuing education credit course
  • Counselling
  • Day school course/program *
  • Full-time employment
  • ILC/correspondence/self-study
  • Independent student credit course
  • Other activities approved by SAL committee
  • Other courses/workshops
  • Part-time employment
  • Training or certifications related to the workplace
  • Volunteering

 

* If a student’s SAL attendance type is more than or equal to 70 minutes, then the student must have this component (as well as at least one Ministry course in their timetable otherwise OnSIS will provide errors for the student).

 

Start date: (required field) Record the date the student started the component.

 

End date:  Optionally record the date the student ended the SAL component (if applicable).

 

Notes:  Optionally record a note about the component.
