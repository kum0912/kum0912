Lunches

When determining the best location to schedule classes, and when assigning students into classes, if the school requires a period to be reserved for a lunch break, the lunch period (or periods) has to be defined.  Lunch periods are optional, and often are not defined if all students share the same lunch period.  For example, if a school has 4 periods for teaching courses and all students have lunch between periods 2 and 3, the school may be setup with 4 periods per day and no lunches.

 

If the school offers multiple lunch periods, they need to be set up before using any automated scheduling tools (like master builder). If the school has a 5 period day, with lunch in either period 3 or 4, then the school would define periods 3 and 4 as lunch periods.  Master builder will take this into consideration when assigning periods to classes, with some classes getting scheduled into period 3 and some classes getting scheduled into period 4.  The automatic assignment of students into classes (load students) will leave either period 3 or period 4 empty every day in the student’s timetable.

 

Sometimes schools offer a class or two over the lunch period.  See Classes offered during lunch (in Important concepts) for different approaches to this.   

 

To set up the school’s lunch periods for next year, choose Scheduling next year > Prepare for next year. Select the GO button on the Lunches card.
