Payments

 

 

When someone gives the school funds for a specific student, those funds are considered payments. Typically, each payment is associated with a specific fee or multiple fees. Sometimes, payments are made that aren’t linked to any fees, and these are called payment credits (e.g. if a payment is made before a fee is applied).

 

If a student owes $5 for a new lock, $20 for an activity fee, and $50 for a field trip, here are some examples of how payments affect fees and outstanding account balances:

 

  • The student pays $5 cash. That $5 is automatically associated with the lock fee and that lock fee is considered paid in full.
  • The student then provides a $30 cheque. That cheque is automatically associated with the full $20 activity fee (so it is paid in full), and it is also used to pay $10 toward the field trip (leaving $40 outstanding for the field trip).
  • The student then provides another cheque for $50. That cheque is associated with the outstanding $40 for the field trip.  The extra $10 payment becomes a payment credit that can be applied later when new fees are added, or it can be refunded.
  • The parent provides an e-fund payment of $100, knowing that the student is going to have hockey fees. That $100 becomes a payment credit because it is not associated with any fees.
  • The school adds the hockey fee of $125.  When the fee is added, it will suggest that the $110 in payment credits is used to pay this fee, and if this is done, the hockey fee balance will be $15.

 

Payments appear on Account Bills and other similar reports as a P, with their respective receipt number.

Since payments should be associated with fees, a Fees card will appear listing all outstanding fees. The sum of these fees will appear in the tool bar. The fees card is used when the amount of the payment is entered.  You'll see these fields:

 

Amount:  The dollar amount of the payment.  If there is a fee matching the amount, it will automatically have a check in the box beside that fee in the Fees card.  If there is no exact match, it will apply the fee to the most overdue fee (if due dates are not used, it will use the fee with the oldest transaction date).  It will apply to as many fees as needed, until the entered payment amount is used up/dispersed. The amount applied to each fee will appear in the ‘Amount to apply’ column so that the sum of this column equals the amount entered in this field. The check boxes and the amount to apply fields may be changed if the automatic association is not the desired distribution of payment.

 

Alternately, if someone wishes to pay some of their fees, but they don’t know the sum, check the boxes of the fees to be paid, adjust the ‘amount to apply’ if only part of a fee is to be paid, then watch the Amount field automatically adjust to the selected fees and amounts.

 

Date:  The date of the payment (it will default today’s date).

 

Payment type:  Select the type from the list of possible types.  Example types might be Cash, Cheque, E-payment, etc.  See Setup payment types for additional information.

 

Receipt number:  The receipt number is automatically assigned, but if needed, it can be changed to any alphanumeric value as long as it is a unique number.  The application will automatically default the next available receipt number based on the starting receipt number set up for the school.  See Setup ….

 

Cheque/Slip number:  If applicable, optionally record a number corresponding to the number on the cheque or other document provided for payment. This can provide useful when tracking down NSF cheques.

 

Comment bank:  (Only available if the school has set up a list of comments (see Setup ….)).  Rather than enter a unique comment in the Comment field, you may optionally select a comment from the school’s list of pre-defined comments.  To use this feature, first select the drop down arrow to pick the desired comment.  Then select the Copy icon located to the right of the field.  The selected comment will then appear in the comment field.

 

Comments:  A comment may be optionally entered when adding a new payment.

 

Note: Payments can be made even if no fees are listed.  See Payment credits for details.

 

Edit a payment

Once a you make a payment you can make changes, but there are some rules:

 

  • Edits to the payment date, payment type, Cheque/Slip No. and comment are permitted.
  • Edits to the amount of the payment are not allowed.  If the payment was reversed due to a non-sufficient funds payment (e.g. NSF cheque), see NSF (Non-sufficient funds) payments. If the amount of the payment was entered incorrectly, void this payment and then create a new payment. See Voiding payments for details.
  • If  the payment resulted in a payment credit where a refund was issued, the payment cannot be voided or considered NSF.  If the refund was issued in error, it may be voided, and then the payment can be voided or considered NSF.

Void a payment

Voids can be used to delete payments and refunds only. Voids will not appear on the student’s record or any reports. There is however an audit trail, and there is a record of this in the database for audit purposes if needed.

 

A payment can be voided by following these steps:

 

• Access the student's record, and select the FEE TRANSACTIONS tab. Row click a fee and select Payment from the menu:

Go to the TRANSACTIONS tab. Select the row that contains the payment.  This will provide a menu of options, including a VOID option which will prompt a message to confirm that the payment is to be voided.  To prevent accidental removal of payments, the Void option is only available from the Transactions view.

 

 

Note:  If  the payment resulted in a payment credit where a refund was issued, the payment cannot be voided.  If the refund was issued in error, it may be voided, and then the payment can be voided.

 

NSF (Non-sufficient funds) payments

If a payment was made by cheque, and there is not enough money in the cheque writer’s bank account to cover the amount, then the cheque is considered NSF. Therefore, the school does not actually receive the funds and the original payment record needs to be reversed so that the student still owes the money.

 

Follow these steps when a payment is considered NSF:

 

• Go to the TRANSACTIONS tab.  Select the row that contains the payment.  This will provide a menu of options.  Select the Edit option.

 

• From the Edit payment form, select the NSF icon in the tool bar.

 

• At the confirmation message, select the REVERSE button:

 

Reversals appear on Account Bills and other similar reports as a N, with their respective receipt number.  Labelling the payment as NSF will create a reversal adjustment transaction as shown here:

  

Note:  If  the payment resulted in a payment credit where a refund was issued, the payment cannot be considered NSF.  If the refund was issued in error, it may be voided, and then the payment can be considered NSF.

 

 

Payment credits

Payment credits displays the total amount of money that is in the student’s account but that money is not yet associated with any fees.  This is typically the result of overpayments that a student has made or if a charge has been edited such to result in a surplus for a student the amount is automatically categorized as a Payment Credit.

As previously mentioned, payment credits are typically the result of overpayments that a student has made or if a charge has been edited such to result in a surplus for a student, the amount is automatically categorized as a Payment Credit.  Here are some further examples of how payment credits are created:

 

  • The student had a class in their timetable with a fee and paid the course fee.  The student then dropped the course within the allowable refund time.  Since the fee was paid already, a payment credit would be automatically created when the class was dropped from the student’s timetable.
  • The student paid a fee.  The amount of the fee was then reduced, or voided.  Since the fee was paid already, a payment credit would be automatically created when the fee amount was reduced.
  • A deposit was paid.  Rather than refunding the deposit, the deposit money was converted to a payment credit.  This is done by selecting the deposit (click the row) and then select the option Convert to payment credit, as shown here:

Using payment credits to pay outstanding fees

Sometimes a student can have both outstanding fees and payment credits.  Although any new fees will suggest that payment credits are used to pay for those new fees, users can choose not to apply the existing payment credits at the time of a new fee.  As well, when adding student fees using the Group fees feature, it does not check for payment credits.

If this scenario exists, an icon will appear in the bottom right corner of the Payment credits card on the student’s summary view.

 

  Select the Apply credits to fees icon to access the form where the payment credits can be associated to the student’s outstanding fees (as shown in earlier in Adding a new fee will suggest that a payment credit is used).

Refunds

Refunds are issued when funds are returned to the student.  Refunds can be issued when:

 

• the student has payment credits (as explained in How payment credits are created)

• the student has deposited money and that can now be returned (see Deposits for details).

 

If no refunds are applicable/available for a student, the refund functionality described here will be hidden.  

 

Hint:  If the student paid a fee, and that payment is to be refunded to the student, you must first void the fee (see Voiding fees).  This will convert the payment into a payment credit which may then be refunded.
