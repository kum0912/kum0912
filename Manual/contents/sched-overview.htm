Scheduling next year - Secondary

Overview

Secondary scheduling creates individual timetables for students based on each student's course selections, as explained here. Elementary scheduling does not use course selections but rather allows unique timetables for each student, although typically a group of students have many or all of the same classes. See Scheduling next year - elementary for more info on this process.

 

edsembli SIS includes powerful scheduling tools to help the secondary school construct the best master timetable for the students and staff attending the school next year.   

 

The master builder tool can automatically schedule classes into their best placement (period or periods). The best placement for each class is the location (period or periods) that has the fewest student conflicts, and where the teacher and room are available.  Master builder takes into consideration the school’s setup for semesters/terms/days/periods, teachers schedules, room schedules, the course selections of the students, as well as whether the school has locked any classes into specific semesters and/or periods.

 

The master schedule (aka next year’s classes) can be saved at any point in the process using the snapshot tool.  As you work toward the final master timetable, you may want to try different scenarios knowing that you can restore a snapshot anytime.

 

In addition to these steps, be sure to read the Important concepts section to help understand terminology (such as preferred vs alternate course selections), how to record new students/staff attending next year or leaving at the end of this year, as well as some overall scheduling concepts.

Print timetables

Once the scheduling process is done, student/teacher/room timetables and lists of students in classes may be optionally printed, or, they can be made available online for students and teachers.  Although online access to next year’s timetables can be enabled, most schools just expose the new school year’s timetables after transferring to current (next step).   

  • Student timetable
  • Teacher timetable
  • Room timetable
  • Class lists

 

Year-end roll over

The year end roll over involves archiving attendance and achievement data (if not done previously), and then running the ‘transfer to current’ process.  This copies the next year’s courses, classes, rooms, homerooms, advisor groups into the corresponding tables for this year, and students will now have their new grades, homerooms, advisor groups, and timetables in ‘this year’.  Access to the options in the Scheduling next year menu should no longer be necessary as all changes should be done in the current year from this point forward.  View access to next year data is provided, but edit access is prohibited.

 

  • Archive attendance & achievement
  • Transfer to current (board/division/district only)
