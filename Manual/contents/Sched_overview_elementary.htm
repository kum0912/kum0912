Scheduling next year - Elementary

Overview

edsembli SIS includes powerful scheduling tools to help elementary schools roll over to a new school year, including grade advancement, assignment of new homerooms, and the addition of classes into the students timetables.

 

Most elementary schools now have classes in the students timetable based on the subjects that appear on the report card.  Elementary students are typically assigned into a homeroom and the homeroom teacher is typically the teacher responsible for the core subjects. The tools for creating classes and assigning student timetables are available for next year (via the Scheduling next year options described here), or for this year if classes are assigned after the year end roll over.  

 

The following outline shows the steps involved during the scheduling process, including the assignment of classes to students to build their next year's timetables. In addition to these steps, be sure to read the Important concepts section to help understand terminology and how to record new students/staff attending next year or leaving at the end of this year. Before following these steps, it is important to know which approach best fits your schools needs. The steps are different depending upon the approach.

 

Approach A:  Classes are created using the ASSIGNED CLASSES feature in homerooms

 

This is the recommended approach for elementary schools where all students in the homeroom have the same timetable (or at least many of their classes are the same).  This approach clears out the classes and then the new classes are created within the homeroom setup. As students are assigned to the homeroom, they are automatically assigned into the classes.  This means that timetables are automatically created for new students, and if a student changes homerooms, their timetable is automatically adjusted.

Approach A involves these steps:

A1. Prepare for next year

A2. Remove all elementary classes

A3. Setup homerooms and their assigned classes

A4. Assign students to homerooms

A5. Define class schedule

A6. Assign other classes to students

A7. Advisor groups (optional)

A8. Print timetables (optional)

A9. Year-end roll over

 

 

Approach B:  Classes are created using the traditional setup of classes

 

With this approach, schools can use the familiar set up of classes to create all the classes for next school year.  Then the 'assign students to classes' tools can quickly add the classes to the student's timetables.  With this approach, student timetables are never automatically updated to reflect the classes that are assigned to the students in the homeroom. As new students are added to the school and they are assigned to a homeroom, the student's timetable will be empty until the classes are added.

 

Approach B involves these steps:

 

B1. Prepare for next year

B2. Setup classes

B3. Setup homerooms

B4. Assign students to homerooms

B5. Assign students to classes

B6. Advisor groups (optional)

B7. Print timetables (optional)

B8. Year-end roll over

 

 

Note: The steps for each approach may be varied to match the school's characteristics and preferences.

 

If the school also includes secondary school grades using traditional high school scheduling features, we would suggest that any constraints on the timetable for these shared elementary and secondary teachers/rooms be added to the classes table so that the secondary scheduling will take them into account.  If there are no constraints, it is possible to leave these classes as floater classes (not assigned a period), and then they can be scheduled around the secondary school subjects.
