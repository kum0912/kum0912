This year vs next year

The school may plan for the next school year without interfering in any way with the operation of the current school year.  This is possible because there is distinct ‘this year’ vs ‘next year’ school information for

 

  • Courses
  • Classes (aka the master timetable)
  • Semester/term/day/period setup

 

Every student and staff member has a school year status that identifies whether the student/teacher is attending the school this year and/or next year.  If the status contains the word ‘next’, the person is expected at the school next year and will be considered in the scheduling processes. Based on these statuses, students attending the school next year will have distinct ‘next year’ information for their:

 

  • Grade
  • Timetable (classes)
  • Homeroom
  • Advisor group


Staff attending next year will have distinct 'next year' information for their assigned:

 

  • Classes
  • Homerooms
  • Advisor groups

Initialize next year prior to doing anything

Before starting to work on anything related to next school year, run the ‘Initialize next year’ process  This step can be done any time in the school year, but typically schools will run it in December/January before the students begin their course selections.  This step will copy over courses, classes, homerooms, and advisor groups from this year into next year, and it will advance the students’ next years grades.  See Scheduling > Prepare for next year > Initialize next year for more information.

 

Add new students/staff

At any time you may add new students and staff to the school. It is important to set the school year status to ‘Next year only’ to reflect the fact that they are only attending your school next year (and not this year).   

 

Students may be added using the options under Student tools > Add new student.

 

Add student:  Use this to manually add one student at a time into the school.

 

Enrol students from this board/division:  Use this to automatically add a group of students from another school who have this school set as their destination school (e.g. to enrol students from the feeder elementary school).

 

Manage online registrations: Use this to automatically enrol students who have entered registration info using the school's online application.

 

Similarly, new staff to the schools may be added using Staff tools > Add new staff > Add staff.  

 

Students and staff who are not returning next year

Set the school year status to “This year only” for those students and staff who are not returning to the school next year.  By doing this, these individuals will not be considered in the scheduling processes.  

 

Course selections

Not applicable to elementary schools.

 

Course selections represent the courses that the student would like to take in the next school year. Each course selection is set as a preferred course or an alternate course.

 

Preferred courses: The courses that the student would prefer to have in their timetable next year.

 

Alternate courses: The courses that the student would like to take if unable to get into a preferred course.

 

Each preferred course has an optional check box for “Can this course be substituted with an alternate?”. The automatic process for creating the students’ timetables (called ‘load students’) has an option to allow the automatic replacement of a preferred courses with an alternate course rather than leave the student with a conflict in their timetable.  This replacement functionality will only happen if the preferred course is set to yes for “can this course be substituted with an alternate”.

 

If the school grants student access to edit course selections online, the school may optionally lock specific courses so that they may not be removed by the student.

 

Hint: Some schools create placeholder courses that are not official courses. For example, if a student wants to complete all their credit requirements in semesters 1, the school may set up a placeholder course called 'NOSEM2' and create a class 'NOSEM2a' that occupies semester 2, all periods. Students with this course selection will have all other courses scheduled into semester 1 since semester 2 is occupied with the placeholder course.

 

Semester/term & day/period sets and patterns  

Not applicable to elementary schools.

 

 

It is imperative that you make sure your semester term and day/period sets and patterns are defined correctly (Scheduling next year > Prepare for next year > Sem/term/day/period).  For example, do not have a set semester/term set called ‘Semestered’ that contains patterns for Sem 1, Sem 2, and Full year.  The automatic scheduler is free to assign a class with the ‘Semestered’ set into any one of those 3 patterns, which is not a desirable outcome.  Different sets would be most appropriate for this scenario (set for ‘semestered’ and a different set for ‘full year’).

Similarly, carefully examine the day/period sets and patterns.  For example, do not have one set defined that has patterns for single periods and double periods in the same set.  If a class is assigned to this incorrectly defined set, classes may end up being assigned in single periods when you really wanted them to be assigned in a double period.  By making these 2 distinct sets, any automatic assignment of a pattern will be limited to only the applicable patterns in that set.  

 

Grouped classes

Not applicable to elementary schools (although this feature is available).

 

Sometimes, a teacher is responsible for teaching more than 1 course during a period (e.g. a grade 10 and 11 physical education class are taught at the same time by the same teacher). Use the ‘Grouped classes’ field (available when editing a class) for this situation to ensure all the classes stay together with the same teacher, room and schedule.

 

Snapshots

Not applicable to elementary schools.

 

A snapshot is a copy of the next year's classes. Snapshots can be created and restored, allowing you to experiment with different class schedules knowing that you can return to saved snapshot at anytime. See Scheduling next year > Classes > Snapshots.

 

Lock class schedules where needed

Not applicable to elementary schools.

 

You may lock the semester/term pattern and/or the day/period pattern for any class by selecting the lock/unlock icon (while editing the class).  If you want to ensure that a class remains in a specific semester, then lock the semester/term pattern.  Similarly, if you want to ensure that a class remains in a specific period, then lock the day/period pattern.  The automatic scheduling tool will not change those setting if they are locked.

 

 

Hint: Some schools create placeholder courses and classes and lock them into specific patterns in order to manipulate where real classes may be scheduled. Here are some examples:

 

  • If a teacher is only available to teach in the morning, assign the teacher to a placeholder class that is locked into the afternoon periods. All other classes for this teacher will be forced into the morning periods.

 

  • If a room is not available during specific periods, assign the room to a placeholder class that is locked into the unavailable periods. (Note: if the class has a special schedule rather than patterns, it will be treated as if it is locked).

 

  • Some schools create PREP classes as placeholders for a teacher's prep period. These are not locked so that the scheduler will find a location in the timetable for the teacher's prep class. These placeholders are not necessary because the automatic scheduler provides a setting for the maximum number of periods/day a teacher can teach. If placeholders are used, it may be necessary to create one placeholder class per teacher per semester (and remember to adjust the value of the maximum period/day in the automatic scheduler so that it allows all periods).

Multiple rooms, multiple schedules, special schedule

Not applicable to elementary schools.

 

There are some advanced features for classes that may apply in some situations.  Please use these special features only where needed, as they may make it difficult for other classes to be scheduled if they are used incorrectly.  These features are typically only used when a master timetable is build completely by hand without the use of any tools, and the timetable is provided to the office for entry into the SIS.

 

 When editing a class, use the MULTIPLE ROOMS tab if the room of the class is not the same every time it is taught.

 

Use the MULTIPLE SCHEDULES REQUIRED button to add another schedule to a class if the schedule is unique (e.g. a full year class is changing from period 1 in semester 1 to period 3 in semester 2).

 

Set the 'Does this class have a special schedule" Yes/No field to Yes if the class schedule is random and doesn’t fit into any definable pattern.

 

See the setup of classes for more info on these features.

 

Classes offered during lunch  

Not applicable to elementary schools.

 

Sometimes a school may offer a few classes over the lunch.  Using an example where most students have 2 regular periods before lunch, and 2 regular periods after lunch, and some students have classes during lunch, here are some approaches to handle classes offered during lunch. The same approach can be applied to classes offered before or after school:

 

Lunch classes do not need to appear in a lunch period and ‘by period’ attendance is not required:

 

If the lunch classes do not need to appear in the timetable in a specific lunch period, and if ‘by period’ attendance is not required for these classes, consider scheduling these classes into no period.  Create a day/period set called floater (or whatever name you’d like to use), with one pattern called floater, and that pattern has no periods selected.  Schedule the lunch classes into the floater set/pattern (by editing the class).  These floater classes will appear as one of the student’s additional classes in their timetable (listed below the regular classes in the timetable grid).  These lunch classes may be flagged as reportable so that achievement information may be recorded for them if appropriate.  These classes will never conflict with other classes and they will never get assigned to any other period by master builder - automatic.   

 

Lunch classes need to appear in the timetable in a specific lunch period and/or ‘by period’ attendance is required:

 

If a specific period is required for lunch classes, this lunch period may be introduced into the schedule at the start of the scheduling process, or at the end of the scheduling process.   

 

  1. Lunch period is introduced at the start of the scheduling process by setting the number of periods per day to include the lunch period (e.g. 5 period per day).  All day/period patterns for regular (non-lunch) classes do not have the lunch period checked off (e.g. no pattern has period 3 checked off).  A day/period set is defined specifically for the lunch period. Create a day/period set called lunch (or whatever name you’d like to use), with one pattern called lunch, and that pattern has period 3 selected.  Schedule the lunch classes into the lunch set/pattern (by editing the class).  These classes will never conflict with regular classes and they will never get assigned to any other period by master builder - automatic.  These lunch classes will automatically appear in a student’s timetable if the student has it in their course selections. NOTE:  do not specify the lunch period in Prepare for next year > Lunches.  If period 3 is added as a lunch period, the students will not be scheduled into these lunch classes because it is reserving period 3 for a lunch break.

  

  1. Lunch may be introduced at the end of the scheduling process if the school would prefer to schedule without the lunch period (e.g. 4 period day).  Once the timetable is done, the school changes to a 5 period day.  All patterns need to be examined carefully and modified as needed.  For example, a pattern for period 4 (under the old 4 period schedule) may need to be renamed to period 5 and the check box for period 4 is removed while period 5 is checked off.  Similarly, the period 3 pattern (under the old 4 period day) may need to be renamed to period 4 and the check boxes for only period 4 are selected.  By changing the patterns, the classes will automatically have the renamed patterns and the timetables will adjust to the new periods.

 

Once all the regular classes are in the new periods, assign the lunch pattern (a set/pattern specifically for period 3) to the lunch classes.  After the schedule is done, add the classes to the student’s timetable using the add classes tool, or by editing the timetables of each student individually.
