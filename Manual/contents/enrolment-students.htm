Student records > Enrolment > Student enrolment



Overview

The Student enrolment option  in a student’s record contains information related to the student’s enrolment in this school and this option is available for all students: present, future and past.

 

You'll find the Student enrolment menu option in the Enrolment submenu.

 

 

Student enrolment is grouped and presented on separate cards titled Student enrolment, School enrolment, and Enrolment reporting, as described below. Many fields in the Student enrolment area show board defined values in drop down lists. Boards can add/expire values in these tables using the options in Setup > Demographics & enrolment.

 

 

Student enrolment: This contains student-specific enrolment data, which remains consistent regardless of the school. Information contained on this card can only be edited by the edsembli record owner. There are slight variations between provinces, but typically it contains the following information:

 

  • student numbers (school student number, provinical student numbers, other numbers if appropriate such as college/university application numbers in Ontario.
  • record ownership to control which school can edit student-specific info (e.g. personal info that stays the same for all schools)
  • Dates: when the record was first created in edsembli SIS, the student's entry date to elementary and secondary school, and the student's entry date to this board/division/district,
  • A yes/no field to indicate the student has alternate programs (which will enable the alternate notes)

 

 

School enrolment: This card contain school-specific enrolment information such as entry/exit info for each time the student enrolls and leaves this school. The info entered here will not impact the student’s information at any other school. Students may have more than one set of entry/exit dates if they return to the school after a period of non-enrolment at the school. This is referred to as 'previous enrolment' (see Returning  students).

 

Enrolment reporting: This card contains info that is typically collected for provincial and other regulatory reporting. In many cases, this information is used for funding purposes.

 

Select Schools

If the student’s record exists in other schools use the drop down arrow to select a different school and see the other school’s enrolment information. Since the student enrolment information is the same for all schools, the ability to select a different school is located after the Student enrolment card (above the School enrolment card). All cards under the selected school will show the information for that school.

 

Enrolment tables

Many enrolment fields use drop down lists to select the student's value. These values are defined by the board/division/district using the options under Setup > Demographics & enrolment. Although every attempt will be made to keep these tables up-to-date in this manual, please refer to the provincial tables for the most up to date information.

 

Alberta only: Links are also provided for some PASI fields that will take you to an edsembli maintained page listing PASI values. For the most accurate table values and rules, please consult the official PASI site.

Internal transfers

As certain fields are changed for a student, the date of the change is recorded in the internal transfers along with the original and new values assigned to the student. These internal transfers may be used when running reports on dates in the past where it is important to report the value the student had on a prior date. Note that internal transfers are related to the school and they are not recorded when a student’s data is changed prior to attending the school (e.g. when the edit is done before the student’s entry date to this school).

 

The internal transfers icon will appear on all cards that include fields that are tracked in the student's  internal transfers. Select the icon to see the student's internal transfers (which are also available from the student's record using Enrolment > Internal transfers).

 

 

 

Student enrolment

The Student enrolment card contains student-specific enrolment information that is the same regardless of which school the student attends. Only the edsembli record holder can edit information contained on this card.  There may be slight variations to the fields on this card, depending upon the province. Use the edit icon if changes are needed.

 

 

 

You can record the following information:

 

Alberta Student Number (ASN): (Alberta only) This is the Alberta provincially assigned student number (9 digits formatted as ####-####-#). When PASI is enabled, the ASN cannot be edited.

 

Ontario Education Number (OEN): (Ontario only) This is the Ontario provincially assigned student number (9 digits formatted as ###-###-###). See Ontario reporting > Ontario Education Numbers > Request OENs from Ministry for more info.

 

Student number: This is the locally assigned student number. This number is assigned when a student is entered into the edsembli SIS for the first time and follows the student throughout his/her career at the board/division/district.  The numbers are determined by the board/division/district using Setup > Board > Student numbers.

 

Ministry Identification Number (MIN): (Ontario only) This field will only appear for Ontario students who were enrolled in the school years ago when MIN numbers were assigned prior to the introduction of OENs.They are formatted as ###-###-###,

 

OUAC number: (Ontario only).  When a student has an application number for university, it is recorded here (9 digits formatted as #########). The OUAC number is really 11 characters long to accommodate with the first 4 characters representing the year. Do not enter the first 2 characters into the field (e.g. if the number is 20191234567, enter 191234567). We recommend that a school record the student's application number as it is still used in the OUAC reporting (see Ontario reporting > Post secondary > Send info to OUAC for more info).

  

OCAS/OUAC SSSN: (Ontario only) This field is automatically populated with the student's OEN. The school specific student number (SSSN) is sent in the files created for the Ontario College Application Service (OCAS) and the Ontario Universities Application Centre (OUAC), and the files will use this number (which is the same as the student's OEN number). If for any reason the student's OEN is changed, this field will not change so that any subsequent files created for OCAS or OUAC will still retain the original number to reference the student.  If OCAS or OUAC makes an inquiry about a student, they will provide this number.  

 

edsembli record owner: Student–specific data is stored once for each student even when the student's record exists in multiple schools in the board/division/district. One school is considered the edsembli record owner, and that school is displayed here. The edsembli record owner is automatically calculated based on the current date and the student’s entry and exit dates at each school.

 

If this school is the edsembli record owner, but another school should really be the record owner, it is recommended that the student's entry and exit dates throughout the board/division/district be reviewed. If it not an issue of data entry, then the Edit icon may be used to switch the edsembli record owner switch from Yes to No. By setting it to no, the next most appropriate school will automatically become the record owner.

 

The edsembli record owner field will impact the profile area of a student’s record. The profile will include one of the following icons:

 

The tool tip will show "This school is the edsembli record owner".

 

The tool tip will show "This school is not the edsembli record owner. Record owner is School Name".

 

Date added to edsembli: This date is automatically recorded when the student is first added into the SIS database. This date cannot be edited and is for informational purposes only.

 

Entry to elementary: This is the date the student entered elementary school. This is typically the date the student first officially started school. Some schools may offer nursery school and for those students, this date may represent a future date when the student is old enough to attend elementary school.

 

Entry to secondary: This is the date the student entered secondary or high school. This is typically the date the student entered or will be entering grade 9 or 10, depending upon the province. This date is used in schools with both elementary and secondary students (e.g. K to 12) to help determine whether the student is included in distinct reports that may be unique for elementary vs secondary students.

 

Entry to board/division: This is the date the student entered this school board/division/district.

 

Alternate programs:  This is a Yes/No field to flag whether the student requires access to alternate program notes.

 

Section 23 eligibility :  (Alberta only). "The Section 23 eligibility code is used to identify students whose parents have a legal right to have their child receive French instruction in accordance with Section 23 of the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms". (PASI information Guide - Student and Enrolment 2.0.pdf, 4.15 Section 23 Eligibility, p.54). This code refers to educational instruction in a francophone education program or school and is not to be confused with French immersion. When a student registers for school, Public, Separate, Charter and Francophone Authorities are required to ask this question “Is this student eligible for French instruction?” and the response is recorded here.

 

School enrolment

The School enrolment card contains information about the student’s enrolment at this school. This includes status information along with entry (aka origin) and exit (aka withdrawal/transfer/retirement) info. Students who leave the school and then return at a later date will have multiple records of origin and retirement information. The student’s current enrolment information will be displayed first, followed by any previous enrolment information where applicable.

 

 

Alberta schools will see additional fields in this area, as required for PASI.

 

 

Use the Edit icon in the School enrolment card if the info needs changes. The edit School enrolment form (shown below) is divided into 3 areas: Overview, Current - entry info, and Current - exit info (which is collapsed if exit info doesn't apply to the student). Some provinces may see additional fields, as explained below, but these fields are not shown in the image.

 

 

 

Overview

 

These are the fields in the Overview section that apply to all schools:

 

School year status: This status provides a quick way to organize students based on when they are attending or have attended this school. Every student is assigned a status reflective of the student’s enrolment at this school. The following statuses are available:

 

    • This year and next year: student is actively attending school this year and is scheduled to attend school next year.

    • This year only: student is actively attending school this year but is not scheduled to attend this school next year.

    • Next year only: student is attending this school next year (e.g. new to the school next year).

    • Withdrawn: student was attending this school year but has withdrawn from this school (formerly referred to as Deleted). Alberta only:  Withdrawn students cannot have a registration status of active or registered.

    • Past: student attended the school in previous years.

 

The school year status is automatically updated to ‘This year only’ for the students in the highest grade when the school commences the scheduling process. The status is also automatically updated to 'Past' for those students who are not returning to the school when the school rolls over to the new school year.

 

Language: This language setting should be set correctly particularly if the school offers multiple languages (e.g. bilingual schools). It provides a way to flag students based on their language (e.g. English or French).

 

These are the fields in the Overview section that apply to Alberta schools only:

 

Registration status: (Alberta only) One of these registration statuses is used to identify the state of the student’s enrolment at the school:

 

    • Active: Registration record is for the current school year and the student is attending classes.

    • Withdrawn: Registration was ended after the student attended classes but prior to the expected registration exit date. *

    • Created In Error: Registration was created in error.

    • Completed: The registration has been ended as the student remained registered for the duration of the registration period.

    • Cancelled: Registration was ended prior to the student attending classes. *

    • Registered : Registration record is for the current school year or upcoming year and the student is not yet attending classes

    • Closed: Registration was ended. This status can only be provided by PASI. *

 

The registration status needs to be updated when significant events occur that would cause the registration status to change. For example, when the student starts to attend classes, the registration status should be updated from Registered to Active.

 

* Overnight an automated routine is run that evaluates the registration status of all students.

 

If a student’s status is Past or Withdrawn, their registration status must be either canceled, closed, completed, created in error or withdrawn. If the registration status is something else, the registration status will be automatically updated as follows:

 

    • If the status is withdrawn or if the student has been completely removed, registration status is set to canceled.

    • If the status is Past, registration status is set to withdrawn.

 

Registration type:  (Alberta only) These registration types are used to record the type of program or courses that the student is taking, such as regular day classes, summer school, etc.

 

C - Continuing education day student

D – Regular day student

E – Continuing education night student

S – Summer school

N – Regular evening student (only applicable prior to fall 2009)*

T – Summer school night student (only applicable prior to fall 2009)*

 

* These codes may appear when looking at past students only

 

 

Current –entry info

 

These are the fields in the Current - entry info section:

 

Entry date to this school: This is the date the student started attending classes at this school. This date may be in a prior school year.

 

If a student previously attended your school, this date represents the most recent date the student returned to this school.

 

Entry description (called Registration entry status for Alberta schools). Select the most appropriate value from the table of entry descriptions. These values describe where the student came from prior to enrolling in this school. These are also sometimes referred to as origin types or enrolment reasons.  In Ontario, some descriptions will change the origin school field to an origin country or province (where the country or province is selected instead of a school).

 

Registration entry status : (Alberta only)  The registration entry status is used to identify the status of a student on entry to the school for the current school year. Follow the link for allowable values.

 

Origin school: If applicable, this is where you can record the school that the student attended prior to attending this school. Start typing the school to see a list of matching schools. Boards/divisions/districts are responsible for setting up the schools using Setup > Schooling info > Schools.

 

 

Current –exit info

 

The current - exit info subheader is collapsed by default for those students whose exit info fields are disabled (e.g.  student's school year status is 'This year and next year' or 'Next year only').  When a student's status is changed to 'Withdrawn', or 'This year only', this section will automatically expanded and the fields are enabled. The exit info fields are enable only if the student's school year status is withdrawn, this year only or past.

 

Exit date: This is the first date where the student is no longer attending classes at this school. Students who are leaving at the end of the current year may have a future exit date. If a student’s exit date is the first day in the school’s calendar, the student did not actually attend school this year.

 

For Alberta schools, when a student leaves the school, the student’s registration status (in the overview subheader) must be either cancelled or created in error.

 

PASI exit date (last attended): (Alberta only) This date is non-editable. It is automatically calculated as  the last day the student attended class. It is the last regular school day or exam day before the exit date. For example, if the exit date is April 15th, then the PASI exist date will be automatically calculated as April 14th. This field only appears for students with a registration status of active, withdrawn, or completed. PASI exit dates do not appear for any past students.

 

Exit description: Select the most appropriate value from the table of exit descriptions. These values describe the reason the student stopped attending classes at this school or where the student is going after leaving this school. These are also sometimes referred to as destination types or enrolment results. In Ontario, some descriptions will change the destination school field to a destination country or province (where the country or province is selected instead of a school).  Alberts schools may use this link to see PASI exit descriptions.:

 

Destination school: If applicable, this is where you can record the school that the student is transferring to after leaving this school. Start typing the school to see a list of matching schools. Boards/divisions/districts are responsible for setting up the schools using Setup > Schooling info > Schools.  In Ontario, some descriptions will change the destination school field to a destination country or province (where the country or province is selected instead of a school).

 

Previous enrolment

Students may have more than one set of entry/exit dates if they return to the school after a period of non-enrolment at the school. This is referred to as 'previous enrolment' and this information is automatically maintained when a student re-enrolls into a school (see Returning  students for additional info on how this is done).

 

Both current and previous enrolment info are displayed on the student's School enrolment card, under the subheader called "Entry/exit activity at School name", as shown here.

 

 

When editing the school enrolment info, the most recent entry info (and exit info if applicable) are considered 'current - entry info' and 'current - exit info'.  All prior entry/exit info appears in a separate area called previous enrolment, shown only when in the edit form.

 

 

 

Although previous enrolment is automatically maintained, if edits are needed, select the row with the enrolment to access the Edit option.  There should not be any reason to add previous enrolment, but an Add icon is provided in the tool bar for this purpose. All previous enrolment dates must be before the current entry date, and cannot overlap other previous enrolment dates.

 

Returning students

The following information applies to those students who have previously attended the school and have since returned to the school. When a student returns to a school, you may add the student as you would any other new student and the program will find the matching student already in your records and you may proceed with the re-enrolment through any of the add students processes. See Add new student, Enrol students from this board/division, and Manage online registrations for more info.

 

Alternately, if you know that the student is already in your current school year's  student records, you may re-enroll the student by changing the student’s school year status (described above) from Withdrawn to one of the returning status: This year and next year, This year only, or Next year only. This will automatically move entry/exit info into the previous enrolment card, enabling the most recent entry info to be recorded in the 'current - entry info' fields. If a past student is returning, use Student tools > Add new student.

 

Enrolment reporting

The Enrolment reporting card contains information that is typically collected for provincial and enrolment reporting. In many cases, this information is used for funding purposes. This card is unique for every province.

Alberta

Grant program and enrolment types are entered for the student using the Enrolment reporting cards shown here:

 

 

Depending upon the selected grant program, additional fields may appear based upon the province’s reporting requirements. For example, if grant program code 230 (French as a second language) is assigned to a student, the Hours of French field will automatically appear for the entry of the number of hours of French instruction. This value is collected by PASI to calculate the entitlement to Federal Government Funding for French Language programs.

 

There are extensive checks put in place to ensure that the data is entered correctly for PASI reporting. If data is not entered correctly, please pay attention to any warning messages that may appear. Here is an example of a warning message that you may see:

 

 

For Alberta, the Enrolment reporting card is used to record Grant programs and Enrolment types.

Grant programs

Grant programs identify the type or course of study in which a student is enrolled, such as home schooling or bilingual education. Not all students have a Grant Program, however if they do, be sure to enter it here as this is related to funding. You can enter a maximum of three grants programs for the student.  

 

Enrolment Types

Enrolment Type codes are used to identify Alberta students for specialized funding or data collection purposes. These codes are used to differentiate various types of enrolments ranging from regular enrolments to exchange students. Not all students have an Enrolment Type, however if they do, be sure to enter as this is related to funding. A maximum of three enrolment types can be entered as appropriate for the student.

 

 

Ontario

Ontario has a number of enrolment reporting fields categorized into subheaders for second language info, additional info for OnSIS, Consent info (optional), and Optional info, as shown in the example below .Please note that different fields appear depending upon the student and school configuration, and only the school that is the edsembli record owner may edit this info. Where the board is responsible for the values in these tables, they should refer to the OnSIS reference tables for the Ministry codes that are used when reporting to OnSIS.  

 

 

 

 

 

Second language info

English as a second language (ESL): Yes/No field to indicate whether the student is in an ESL program.

 

English literacy development (ELD): Yes/No field to indicate whether the student is in an ESL program.

 

French/Anglais program:  Select the French program (for students attending English schools) or Anglais program (for students attending French schools). When a program is entered for an elementary student, the Minutes/day field appears to record the average number of minutes of instruction the student receives per day.  This field is repeated if the student's language is French and the school is French or Bilingual so that a second Anglais program and corresponding minutes/day (for elementary students) may be entered. Values should be based on the OnSIS reference table values SCND_LANG_PGM_TYPE. There are extensive checks put in place to ensure that the data is entered correctly for OnSIS. If data is not entered correctly, please pay attention to any warning messages that may appear.

 

Indigenous program:  (Elementary students only) Select an indigenous program, if applicable. When a program is entered, the Minutes/day field appears to record the average number of minutes of instruction the student receives per day. Values should be based on the OnSIS reference table values SCND_LANG_PGM_TYPE.

 

 

Additional info for OnSIS

 

Ontario Youth Apprenticeship Program: Yes/No field to indicate whether the secondary school student is in the Ontario Youth Apprenticeship Program (OYAP).

 

Section 23 residence type: (CTCC schools only) Optionally select a residence type for a student in a CTCC school. Values should be based on the OnSIS reference table values CARE_TREATMENT_RES_TYPE

 

Designate school: (CTCC schools only) When a student is attending a CTCC school, another school must be selected as the designate school (e.g. typically the school the student was attending before enrolling in the CTCC school). The designate school's name will appear on all Ministry reports generated by the CTCC school.

 

Mature student: Yes/No field to indicate whether the secondary school student is considered a mature student. Refer to your board definition of a mature student  (typically a student who is at least 18 years of age on or before December 31 of the school year in which he or she returns to school, who was not enrolled in a day school program for a period of at least 10 consecutive months who is re-enrolled in a secondary school program).

 

FNMI declaration: For indigenous students, select the student's  FNMI Declaration and Self ID date.  These fields are also available from the Personal > Indigenous option in the student's record.

 

 

Consent info (optional)

 

These fields are optional and may be interpreted however the board chooses.

 

Consent for school programs: Yes/No field.

 

Consent for walking trips: Yes/No field.

 

Consent for OSR gathering of info including MFIPPA/PHIPA: Yes/No field.

 

Consent of publication of student info (name, photo, work) in: Print, Electronic, News media:  Three Yes/No fields for Print, Electronic, and News media.

 

 

Optional info

 

These fields are optional and may be interpreted however the board chooses.

 

Cohort year: Secondary students may have a cohort year representing the school year the student started grade 9. The values in the list are automatically updated each new year to show 1 year beyond the current year (e.g. if it is currently the 2018/19 school year, table goes to 2019-2020).  

 

Tax support: Select Public (English), Public (French), Separate (English), Separate (French).

 

Literacy component verified: Yes/no field to indicate whether the school has verified the student's Ontario Secondary School Literacy requirement. 

 

Instructional setting: Select from the board defined list of values.

 

Instructional date: Date field.

 

Intends to apply for OSAP (OCAS/OUAC): Select Other, Yes or No from the drop down list.
