Attendance reports: Common features

 

Overview

The attendance applications share many common features. Attendance information is typically presented in “cards” and the same cards are found throughout the attendance options. We recommend that you familiarize yourself with these.

 

When accessing attendance reports, some reports will condense the attendance information so that you can see the attendance in a meaningful, easy to read format. This section will explain how the attendance recorded for a student is abbreviated on some reports.

Abbreviation of AM/PM attendance codes

In order to make the report easy to read, the student’s AM/PM attendance is displayed as follows:

 

. Present for the day

A Absent for the day (morning and afternoon)

A/ Absent for the morning only

/A Absent for the afternoon only

AL Absent for the morning, late for the afternoon

 

Students attending only the morning or only the afternoon will be represented with just one code.

  

Students with the ‘by period’ attendance method can have many different attendance codes entered for a single day, making it unrealistic to present that detailed information on some reports. Sometimes, the period attendance is reported in the morning/afternoon format. See ‘By period’ attendance method – conversion to AM/PM.

Non-school days

Non-school days defined in the school’s calendar will appear as follows:

 

pa Special Day (e.g. professional activity day)

gr Grant Day or General Absence Day in Ontario (e.g. unexpected school closure)

h Holiday (e.g. Statutory holiday)

b Board Day (e.g. holiday designated by the board/division)

 

If a student has no classes on a regular school day, no attendance is recorded. This may be part-time student who are not scheduled for classes on certain days in their timetable, or half-time students who attend school on alternate days. Those days will appear like this:

 

n or N Non-Instructional Day (student is not scheduled for instruction)

 

Other codes you may notice:

 

* Student is not enrolled in the school yet

_ Future date

Ontario schools please see Notes for Ontario schools regarding the use of the * code

Attendance codes

Some reports may give the option to use either the school defined attendance codes or the official attendance codes as shown here:

 

  

School codes: This will show the exact attendance code (A –Z) assigned to the student.

 

Official codes: This will show official codes only (e.g. S – Sick recorded for a student will show as A – Absent). Each school attendance code has an equivalent official code to indicate whether the code is considered Present, Absent, Late, Suspended, or Grant day (for unexpected school closures due to weather, infrastructure issues).

 

Note: By period students may not have this option available if the report is converting the attendance to AM/PM as explained in ‘By period’ attendance method – conversion to AM/PM.

‘By period’ attendance method – conversion to AM/PM

Students whose attendance is recorded for each period may have their attendance converted to AM/PM attendance for some reports. For example, if M = Medical, F = Field trip, T = team sport event, L = Late, and P = Present, and the student has 8 periods in the day, here’s an example of what the attendance might look like for the 8 periods:

 

MPFF PPLT

This type of detailed period information report is difficult to print in limited space so the above example, would be converted to L/P (late to school in morning because of the medical appointment, and Present for the afternoon). The resulting AM/PM attendance will only use the official codes, and therefore, the option to print school attendance codes is not available for those with the ‘by period’ method.

 

This is done by considering the ‘first afternoon period’ defined in the setup of the school’s attendance parameters. If a school has 4 periods, and the first afternoon period is set to period 3, here are some examples of how the attendance is converted to morning/afternoon (A = absent, P = present, L = late and AA AA represent A in period 1, A in period 2, A in period 3 and A in period 4):

 

Attendance in periods 1 to 4 Converted to AM/PM Attendance report
AA AA A/A (absent in the morning, absent in the afternoon A
AA PP A/P (absent in the morning, present in the afternoon) A/
LP PP  L/P (late in the morning, present in the afternoon) L/
AP PP L/P (late for the morning because they attended some of the morning but were late L/
PA AA P/A (present for the morning because they attended some of the morning) /A
AA AP A/L (late for the afternoon because they attended some of the afternoon) AL
PL PL P/P (student wasn’t considered late to the morning or afternoon since they were present at the start of the am and pm) .

 

Notes for Ontario schools

The following information pertains to Ontario schools only. Schools and boards should be familiar with the official Ministry enrolment register instructions for elementary and secondary schools that are updated annually on the Ministry’s site http://www.edu.gov.on.ca/eng/policyfunding/forms.html.

Supervised Alternative Learning (SAL)

The Ministry’s instructions state: For pupils who are participants in a SAL program, record their attendance in the Daily Attendance Record with “G” for the period when they are participating in the non-academic components of the SAL program.

 

In edsembli SIS, the student’s attendance type (in the student's record in Enrolment > Enrolment register info) must be set to SAL. SAL students have one of the following SAL attendance types that determine how the student is reported:

 

  • Less than 70 minutes of day school instruction: These students will see a ‘g’ (General absence day) on the report.

  • More than or equal to 70 minutes of day school instruction: These students will see a ‘g’ (General Absence Day) for half the day, and the attendance entered for the classes in the student’s timetable will be reported for the other half of the day (as per ‘By period’ attendance method – conversion to AM/PM).

Admission of a pupil to the register

If a student is absent at the start of the school year, the Ministry requires * (not enrolled) until the first time the student shows up to school. The Ministry’s instructions state:

 

Enter a pupil’s name on the register on the day on which the pupil actually begins to attend classes for the current school year. Although pupils’ names from the previous school year may be stored in a school’s computerized system for administrative purposes, a pupil’s name must not be entered into the current register until the pupil actually begins to attend classes. Exceptions to this requirement are the following pupils, whose names may be entered on the register on the first day of school for the current school year:

 

  • A pupil who is continuing in a Supervised Alternative Learning (SAL) program from the previous school year with the formal approval of the board’s SAL Committee.

 

In edsembli SIS, if a student’s attendance type is SAL, the student is admitted to the register on the first day of school.

 

  • A pupil who is continuing to receive home instruction.

 

In edsembli SIS, schools are expected to record the attendance of a secondary pupil who is receiving home instructions as if he or she were attending classes at the school. Should the student remain on the register from one school year to the next, and if the student were absent on the first day of school, the school can indicate they have had contact with the student as explained next.

 

  • A pupil who was referred to an attendance counsellor in the previous school year and whose file is still active.  

 

In edsembli SIS, if the student’s contact note on the first day of school has Yes indicated in the ‘Contact with pupil’ field, the student is considered enrolled, and therefore the student's attendance register will reflect the student’s actual attendance (e.g. A rather than *).
