OnSIS > Submission period > Other info

 

 

The Other info option will appear for the following submission periods:

  

  • October secondary school
  • March secondary school
  • June secondary school

 

This option is used to create batch files, using the CREATE button. The batch file is uploaded to OnSIS through the OnSIS web site.  Once OnSIS processes the batch file, a results file is created. The results file is downloaded from OnSIS and is then processed using the PROCESS RESULTS button.  

 

This section describes the Other info batch files. See the OnSIS Important concepts section for info on Updating data in OnSIS using the ‘Create/Upload/Process’ cycle.  

 

The file or files in this option provide additional information required as part of a school’s OnSIS submission.  For secondary schools, this includes students who are not enrolled in the school but have earned their diploma during the submission period (e.g. by completing volunteer hours or SHSM certificates after earning all the required credits in a previous submission or earning the required credits during summer school and the home school grants the diploma).   

 

In March, OnSIS collects info about the courses that a student has selected for the next school year (aka student option sheets) along with the list of all the courses that students may make their selections from (aka the course calendar).

The OnSIS batch file options are detailed below. Prior to selecting the CREATE BATCH FILE option, determine whether other options on the form should be selected.   

 

Purge:  Y/N field. See Important concepts – Purge check boxes for more info.

 

Purge and pre-populate all non-enrolled student info: Y/N field. If set to yes, it will include a command in the batch file that instructs OnSIS to purge and prepopulate all data for students who were submitted to OnSIS but these students did not actively attend school during this submission period.  This will purge and pre-populate diploma info, and option sheets for these non-enrolled students. It will also purge the SHSM program and certification info, and since this SHSM info is on the Student info card, it is important to remember to resubmit this file if you check this setting.  

 

Save results to CSV file (aka Also create CSV file): Y/N field. If set to Yes, the CREATE BATCH FILE option will create the requested batch file(s) and the contents of the batch file(s) will also appear in a comma delimited file (same file name as the XML batch file but with a CSV extension). Some boards/schools generate these csv files to perform data analysis on the information sent to OnSIS.

 

Diplomas for non-enrolled students batch file

 

Default file name: S13_######_NonEnrolStuDiploma_yyyymmdd_hhmm.xml

 

The diplomas for non-enrolled students batch file contains information about students who have been granted a diploma during the submission period, but the student is not part of the school’s submission (e.g. the student is not in the student school enrolment file, therefore the student is considered “non-enrolled”). A non-enrolled student may be granted a diploma by their home school upon completion of summer school or completion of community hours.  Past students will be included if a diploma is granted during this submission period.  Diploma info, including community involvement hours and the Ontario scholarship flag are all recorded in the Achievement > Graduation requirements option of the student’s record.

Course calendar and student option sheets batch file

Applies to the March secondary submission for publicly funded schools only

 

Default file name: S11_######_OptShtt_yyyymmdd_hhmm.xml

 

Every March, OnSIS collects information regarding the courses offered at the school for the forthcoming school year (course calendar), along with the course options selected by the students. The course calendar uses the courses entered into the next year's courses file (Setup > Class info > Courses (next year)).  Each student's course options are taken from the student's course selections (student’s record > Class info > Course selections).  Only the student’s preferred courses are reported to OnSIS. Alternate courses are not reported.

 

Sometimes schools create fictitious course codes for timetabling purposes. If a course is to be excluded from the OnSIS reporting, in the setup of the course (using Setup > Class info > Courses (next year)), check the box called “Exclude from OnSIS submission”.  It will also automatically exclude any courses that have a department defined with a ministry code of 0 (zero).  A warning message will appear on the printout after the batch file is created informing the user of all of the courses excluded from the batch file.

 

The Ministry recognizes that students may register for programs in the spring, but the actual course codes the student will ultimately receive in their fall timetables are not always known this early in the planning process. For this reason, the Ministry has a list of placeholder courses that they will accept in the OnSIS batch files for course options only. The list of placeholder courses is part of the Ministry's reference tables. Here are some examples:

 

ALP1X or ALP2X or ALP3X or ALP4X = Alternative Program, Grade 9 or 10 or 11 or 12

 

APR1X or APR2X or APR3X or APR4X = OYAP, Grade 9 or 10 or 11 or 12  

 

COP1X or COP2X or COP3X or COP4X = CO-OP, Grade 9 or 10 or 11 or 12

 

IBP1X or IBP2X or IBP3X or IBP4X = International Baccalaureate Program, Grade 9 or 10 or 11 or 12

 

OOO4X = Dual Credit:  College-delivered, Apprenticeship  

 

OTH1X or OTH2X or OTH3X or OTH4X = Other, Grade 9 or 10 or 11 or 12

 

RCR1X or RCR2X or RCR3X or RCR4X = Credit Recovery, Grade 9 or 10 or 11 or 12

 

REG1X or REG2X or REG3X or REG4X = Regional Program, Grade 9 or 10 or 11 or 12

 

SCE1X or SCE2X or SCE3X or SCE4X = School to Career, Grade 9 or 10 or 11 or 12

 

SLP1X or SLP2X or SLP3X or SLP4X = Supervised Alternative Learning (SAL), Grade 9 or 10 or 11 or 12

 

SSR1X or SSR2X or SSR3X or SSR4X = Student Success, Grade 9 or 10 or 11 or 12  YYY4X = Dual Credit:  College Delivered, College Course.   

 

There is no extract option for this batch file.  The file sets the flag to automatically purge all previously submitted course calendar and student option information prior to processing the contents of the batch file.
