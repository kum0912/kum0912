Deposits

 

Students may also have funds that are submitted to the school for deposits on items they are borrowing from the school.  For example, some schools require text book deposit funds that are collected at the start of the course and the funds are returned to the student when they return the text book to the school. These student deposits may also be tracked in edsembli.

 

The school creates a list of deposit fee types to track the required deposits (see Setup ___).  Once 1 or more deposit fee types are set up, the student’s fees will show a tab called DEPOSITS.  This tab shows all activity associated with the student’s deposits. The DEPOSITS tab may include up to 2 cards to convey the student’s deposit information:  Deposit balance card (every student will have this), and a Deposits card.

 

Note: the deposit information may include references to due dates. Schools can choose to track due dates on every fee, including deposit fees. When a fee is not paid by the due date, it is considered overdue.  If a school does not track due dates, any references to due dates will be disabled.

Deposits card

The Deposits card shows the deposit history including Date, Due, Fee type, Invoice number, Deposit required (amount), Payment (if any), Balance (if any) and Comments (if any).

 

 

Row clicking a deposit lets you view Details, Add, Edit and Refund a deposit, as well as convert the deposit in to a credit if a refund is not wanted.

 

Details

The standard Details option is available to provide a detailed view of information, without going into the Edit interfaces. Row click and select Details.
