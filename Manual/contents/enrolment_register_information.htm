Overview

 

This option is only available to Ontario schools.

 

The Enrolment register info option only applies to Ontario students as it contains information about the student that influences the school’s enrolment registers. The Ontario Ministry has complex reporting requirements around student enrolment as explained in their two annual Ministry publications, and the SIS has extensive details for setting up classes and students each official register. Follow the links in the chart for additional info on the registers.

The info recorded in this area of the student’s record is used when generating official enrolment registers and when reporting the student to OnSIS for funding. To help simplify the data entry, this area will have variations between different students and school types.  

 

Choose Enrolment > Enrolment register info from the submenu.

 

 

When you select Enrolment register info, the form will show up to 3 cards, depending upon the student:

 

Register info

Full-time equivalent (FTE)

Average daily enrolment (ADE)

 

Only the first card has fields that may be entered. The other cards show the student’s FTE and ADE calculations for today and the Ministry’s count dates (October 31, March 31, June 30). FTE and ADE values are calculated for regular and high credits.  The cards with these calculations include a details area which may be expanded to show some explanations on how these values are calculated, along with some quick links to other area of the student’s record that may influence these calculations.

 

Ontario schools should be familiar with the terms FTE, ADE, and High Credit. The Ministry has different funding formulas for regular FTE, regular ADE, high credit FTE and high credit ADE. The SIS will automatically calculate these values for each student.  A basic explanation of these terms is provided at the end of this page.

Full-time equivalent (FTE)  

The Full-time equivalent (FTE) card will show the student’s Regular FTE, High credit FTE and Total FTE for today, October 31, March 31 and June 30. These values are automatically calculated.

 

 

If you are unsure how FTE works, expand the Details area to read some informational text about these calculations. It will also provide buttons to quickly access other areas of the student’s record that may impact the FTE calculations and enrolment registers.

 

 

This card will never appear for continuing education and summer schools as FTE only applies to regular, e-learning and CTCC schools.

 

The following students will NOT get the Full-time equivalent (FTE) card:

 

  • Student who are past or next year only.
  • Students with a student board residence status Ministry code: 00 – exclude from all Ministry reports; 08 – shared student; 09 - e-learning student.
  • Students who are taking only classes setup to appear in the Independent Study and e-Learning Register for Day School Pupils. These classes are on the Average Daily Enrolment (ADE) card.
  • Students in grade 8 or lower in a private or native school.
  • Students in grade 9 or higher in a non-inspected private or native school.

 

 

Average Daily Enrolment (ADE)  

There are 2 unique presentations of the Average Daily Enrolment (ADE) card, depending upon the type of school:

 

  • Average Daily Enrolment (ADE) for continuing education & summer schools
  • Average Daily Enrolment (ADE) for regular/e-learning/CTCC schools *

 

* This card will only appear for students with classes that appear on the Independent Study and e-Learning Register for Day School Pupils.

 

Average Daily Enrolment (ADE) for continuing education & summer schools

The Average Daily Enrolment (ADE) card, for students attending continuing education and summer schools, will show the students Regular ADE total as of today. Since ADE funding is based on lessons/work units marked (for correspondence/self-study/e-learning classes) and sessions recommended for funding (for all other classes), it will consider the lessons/work units/session info up to today’s date.  The ADE calculations are by class, but they are summed to show the total for the student.  Continuing education ADE is not differentiated between regular and high credit ADE. It will all appear as regular ADE.

 

 

 

If you are unsure how ADE works, expand the Details area to read some informational text about these calculations. It will also provide buttons to quickly access other areas of the student’s record that may impact the ADE calculations and enrolment registers.

 

 

This card will never appear for regular, e-learning, CTCC schools.

  • Student who are past or next year only.
  • Students with a student board residence status Ministry code: 00 – exclude from all Ministry reports; 09 - e-learning student.
  • Students in grade 8 or lower in a private or native school.
  • Students in grade 9 or higher in a non-inspected private or native school.

 

Average Daily Enrolment (ADE) for regular/e-learning/CTCC schools

The Average Daily Enrolment (ADE) card will show the student’s Regular ADE, High credit ADE and Total ADE for today, October 31, March 31 and June 30. These values are automatically calculated.

 

 

If you are unsure how ADE works, expand the Details area to read some informational text about these calculations. It will also provide buttons to quickly access other areas of the student’s record that may impact the ADE calculations and enrolment registers.

 

 

This card will never appear for continuing education and summer schools as it only applies to regular, e-learning and CTCC schools.

  

 

FTE, ADE, High Credit terminology

 

Ontario schools should be familiar with the terms FTE, ADE, and High Credit. The Ministry has different funding formulas for regular FTE, regular ADE, high credit FTE and high credit ADE. The SIS will automatically calculate these values for each student.  Here is a basic explanation of these terms:

 

  1. FTE (full-time equivalent): For many students attending regular publicly funded schools (e.g. regular elementary and secondary students), the Ministry funds a student based on the amount of school instruction the student receives. A full-time student has 210 minutes or more of classroom instruction per school day. A full-time student has an FTE of 1.0.  In the SIS, the student’s attendance type is set to full-time.  

 

If a student has less than 210 minutes of classroom instruction per school day, in the SIS, the student’s attendance type is set to part-time.  The SIS will calculate the student’s FTE value, as a proportion of instructional time compared to a full-time student. The calculation is based on the average daily minutes of instruction divided by the average daily total number of minutes of instruction offered with the school. As a simple example, a secondary student taking 2 courses in a semester when full time students take 4 courses, the student would have a 0.50 FTE (e.g. 150 minutes/ 300 minutes offered).  

 

If a student were taking 3 courses instead, the student would have a 0.75 FTE. If the FTE calculation is equal to or greater than 0.70, the student is considered full-time with an FTE of 1.00.

 

Since FTE is based on the student’s timetable, as the student’s timetable changes, it is possible that the student’s FTE is affected.   

 

  1. ADE (Average daily enrolment):  ADE values are calculated for students taking continuing education and summer school classes and programs.  The individual class/program ADE calculations are summed for the student. The funding for correspondence/self-study classes = # of lessons/work units marked x 0.00579. The funding for all other classes/programs = # of sessions recognized for funding x hours per session /950.

 

ADE is automatically set to 0.00 if the student is also attending a regular day school full-time, or if the student is part-time taking the same courses in a day school.

 

ADE is automatically set to 0.00 if the student is part-time taking the same course(s) in a day school.

 

Continuing education ADE is not differentiated between regular and high credit ADE. It will all appear as regular ADE.

 

ADE, rather than FTE, is also used for classes that appear on the Independent Study and e-Learning Register (for regular day schools/e-learning schools/CTCC schools). Only these classes can have regular ADE and high credit ADE.

 

The calculation of average daily enrolment and high credit factors is available in the Education Act, Ontario Regulation 286/18. For 2018, see:

https://www.ontario.ca/laws/regulation/180286#BK15

 

  1. High credit: Regular FTE calculations apply to classes where the student’s total credits (earned + projected credits based on timetable on the count date) are 34 credits or less. High credit FTE applies to eligible students with over 34 credits. High credit FTE receives different funding than regular FTE. ESL, ELD, ALF, PANA, and credits earned at private schools are excluded from the credit count. The following students are exempt from the high credit FTE calculations:

 

    • Students with an IEP
    • Students in grade 9 or lower
    • Students participating in SAL
    • Students 21 or older as of December 31
    • Students starting grade 9 in an Ontario secondary school who are attending their 4th or lower year of secondary school
    • Students who did not start grade 9 in an Ontario secondary school and are less than 18 years old on December 31
