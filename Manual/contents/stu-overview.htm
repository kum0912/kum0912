Student record access

There are 2 ways to access a student’s record from the dashboard:

 

  •  select the Menu icon in the left corner, then select Student records from the menu.

 

 

  • select the student records icon from the student records card located on the dashboard

 

The select student page will appear using either method.

 

 

Select a student

Enter as much or as little of the student’s name as you want then select the search button or Enter key. For example, enter ‘B’ for a list of students whose surnames start with B, or enter ‘Babcock’ for a list of students whose surnames start with Babcock. If you know the student’s usual name (e.g. of Joshua’s usual name is Joe), enter ‘Babcock, Joe’ to go directly to the student record. Your search may show multiple students matching the search name, so make sure you select the right student.

 

Hint: enter % for a list of all students.

Menu

The student's record represents a single access point for the information recorded for the student. The information is broken down into areas based on the type of information recorded for the student. Using the menu on the left, the content will change to reflect the menu option selected.

 

The student records menu can be hidden or shown by selecting the icon in the top right corner of the student's profile. The same icon is now used for show vs hide, however the tool tip will correctly indicate whether selecting the icon will hide the menu, or show the menu. Note: this icon will appear in the toolbar on smaller devices.

Use this icon to show or hide the student record menu and profile area.
  
Student records has an advanced search feature that lets you search for a specific area in the student’s record (e.g. locker).
Enter criteria beside the Search icon and results will automatically display.



For further information on menus, see Menus in Common Features.

App bar

The App bar for Student records appears across the top of the student’s record. Use the Main menu icon in the top left corner to navigate to other areas of edsembli SIS (e.g. leave the student records area).

 

There are some convenient icons in the top right corner:

 

The Select student icon calls up the Select Student screen.
   
The Previous student and Next student icons move (step) to the next or previous student in the list of resulting students (e.g. the students matching the search criteria used to find this student).
   
  The New window icon lets you keep your existing browser window (tab) open while opening a new browser tab with 3 possible options.  The options will appear when the icon is selected as shown here:
  

  

New student: takes you to the select student interface where a new student may be selected

Dashboard:  takes you to the initial dashboard page that appears when you first log into the SIS

Duplicate student:  creates another browser session with the same student information (creates a duplicate of the page you were looking at). This is handy because you can have multiple student records open at the same time or look at different parts of the same student's record at the side by side..

  
Hint: browser tabs can be maximized or restored down to smaller sizes so that they can be repositioned side by side.


Profile

The student Profile area contains the student’s picture, and other pertinent information for easy reference. Pictures are usually imported annually, but if a student’s picture is not available or needs updating, click the student picture area to select an image from your source.

 

Hint: to quickly see the student photo in its full saved size, right click a student’s picture, then choose Open image in a new tab. This works only in Chrome browser.

 

The profile area shows the student’s usual names and legal names. Usual names appear first and are formatted as Usual Name and Surname. Legal names are displayed second, in a smaller font, and are formatted as legal surname, given name + middle name(s) + , suffix (if applicable).

 

Student flags (coloured circles) appear under the student’s picture. Students may be flagged with a school defined flag (colour, optional flashing, title) using the Flags menu option under Enrolment. Hovering over the flag will show the title assigned to the flag.

 

 

Status is a quick way to organize students based on when they are attending or have attended this school. Every student is assigned a status reflective of the student’s enrolment at the school being accessed. The following statuses are available

This year and next year: student is actively attending school this year and is scheduled to attend school next year. *
  
This year only: student is actively attending school this year but is not scheduled to attend this school next year. *

Next year only: student is attending this school next year (e.g. new to the school next year). Next year only students show next year grade and homeroom in profile
  
Withdrawn: Student was attending this school year but has withdrawn from this school (sometimes referred to as Deleted).
  
Past: Student attended the school in previous years.
  
* statuses are automatically updated to ‘This year only’ for the students in the highest grade when the school commences the scheduling process, and students not returning to the school are updated to 'past' at the end of the scheduling process.

Additional profile area student information:

 

Provincial student number: (field name changes depending on your province e.g. ASN for Alberta).

Student No.: Local student number for board/division/district identification.

Grade: student’s current grade this school year

Homeroom: student’s current homeroom designation, room number and teacher(s)

Birthdate and age (age in years will also include months for students aged 16 and younger).

Gender: Male or female or other gender identifications if applicable

Def. Chrs.: each student has 4 defined characters, represented by 4 0’s if not entered for the student. The school defines what the 4 characters represent and they are “secret” to the school in that only school staff know what they mean. Each defined character may be a may be a letter A to Z, or a number 0 to 100. They are used as a quick means to identify groups of students. For example, a bilingual school may use defined character 1 to represent whether the student is English or French. Each student will have an E or F recorded in the 1st defined character.

Every student will have one of two edsembli record owner icons (see below). As students move between schools in your board/district, the student is still considered a single person with a single student record; however, the individual pieces of information collected for the student are considered either “student” or “school” data.
  
Student data is stored once for the student and is shared automatically with all attended schools. One school is considered the edsembli record owner and only that school may edit that data. Example student data includes name, birthdate, address, contacts, student numbers.

The school is the edsembli record owner and the student’s profile will include this icon. The tool tip will show "This school is the edsembli record owner".
   
The school is not the edsembli record owner and the student’s profile will include this icon. The tool tip will show "This school is not the edsembli record owner. Record owner is School Name".
  
School data is stored uniquely for each school and data may be edited regardless of whether or not the school is the record owner. Each school may edit the data as required. Example school data includes grade, class info, school enrolment info, lockers, notes.

If a student is actively enrolled in this school as well as another school, this icon lets you know that the student is enrolled in multiple schools concurrently. The tool tip will show "Student also enrolled at School Name(s)".

Content

Now that you understand the profile and app bar, the main content area of the student’s record is where the student’s data will appear as you navigate through different menu options.

 

Refer to the specific sections of the manuals for details on each menu option, however, here is a brief overview. Please keep in mind that your security rights will determine whether or not you have view or edit access to specific areas of the student’s record.

Student records menu

Summary: This is a quick overview of the most commonly accessed information for a student. The DETAILS button on each card provides a quick link to more detailed information by taking you to that specific area in the student's record.

 

Personal: This is information that tends to be student-specific, in other words, information that is the same regardless of what school the student attends. Only the school that is considered the edsembli record owner can edit this information. The Personal option has the following submenu options:

 

Name: Displays the student's usual (preferred) and legal names, history of name changes, and aliases (AKA = also known as). Alberta schools can also flag students that have information disclosure restrictions here.

 

Demographics: Displays birthdate, documents that prove a student's birthdate upon registration, citizenship, country of birth, and gender.

 

Contact info: Displays student’s contact information including phone numbers, email addresses, addresses (past, present, future addresses, bus pickup/drop off addresses, and if needed, distinct mailing vs physical vs permanent addresses), and contact details (with respect to Canada’s anti spam legislations).

 

Indigenous: Displays information for federal reporting on indigenous students.

 

Family and contacts: This option has the following submenu options:

 

Contacts: Phone numbers, email addresses, and current address information for the student’s contacts (parents, custodians, guardians, emergency contacts, babysitter, etc). Each contact is given a contact type (e.g. mother), and contacts are flagged if they are the student’s legal guardian, custodian or non-custodial parent (with or without access to student data). Contact types may be duplicated (e.g. 2 mothers), and contact details with respect to Canada’s anti spam legislation are tracked. Only the school that is considered the Maplewood record owner can edit this information.

 

Custody: Contact cards will appear for all contacts that are flagged as a legal guardian, custodian, or non-custodial parent. Selecting a contact card will take you directly to that contact in the Contacts option.

 

Siblings: Displays names of the students in the school who are siblings to this student. Siblings, or other related students (e.g. foster children) may be added or removed. Siblings may be linked even if they do not live in the same household.

 

Class Info: This option has the following submenu options:

 

Classes: The Classes option provides a single location in which to see all the classes in which the student is enrolled in either this year or next year.

 

Timetable: The Timetable option presents the student’s classes organized by periods, days, terms and semesters. Each school’s setup defines the number of semesters, terms in a semester, days in cycle, and periods per day.

 

Course selections:  The courses that a secondary student would like take in the next school year.

 

Attendance: This option has the following submenu options:

 

Summary:  Displays a summary of the student's attendance.

  

Attendance: Displays detailed info about the student's attendance at this school.

 

Notes: Displays the student's attendance notes.

 

Enrolment: This option has the following submenu options:

 

Student enrolment: The Enrolment menu option in a student’s record contains information related to the student’s enrolment in this school, and all other schools the student has or will be attending in the board/division.

 

Enrolment register info:  (Ontario only). Provides access to the info that influences how the student appears in the Ministry's enrolment registers, and provides FTE and ADE statistics.

 

Supervised Alternative Learning (SAL):  (Ontario only) Provides access to info about a student's involvement in a SAL program (if applicable).

 

Flags: Student flags (coloured circles) appear under the student’s picture in the profile area, along with the student’s defined characters, as explained in the Profile area.

 

Schools: This will show a record of the student’s schooling at this board/division/district. All students will have this card, and the contents of the card will be the same regardless of which school accesses the information. If the school is the record owner, this is also where the school can request enrolment at another school in the board/division/district. By doing this, the other school can see the request and accept the student’s enrolment. This is typically used to register a student into a summer school, because the school making the request will remain as the record owner.

 

OSR request: Most schools have an Official Student Record (OSR) established for each student who enrolls in their school.  The OSR is typically a file folder containing paper copies of pertinent data for the student. This option may be optionally used to track the movement of this file.

 

Internal transfers: As certain fields are changed for a student, the date of the change is recorded in the internal transfers along with the original and new values assigned to the student. The information tracked through internal transfers may be used when running reports on dates in the past where it is important to report the value the student had on a prior date.

 

 

Achievement: This option has the following submenu options:

 

Marks and comments:  Provides access to marks and comments recorded by the teachers for this year's report cards.  If access is granted to the detailed markbook info, this information is also available.

 

Graduation requirements: Provides an area where you can indicate what diploma a student is working towards (e.g. their graduation requirements), and schools then you can monitor the student’s completed courses in relation to the course categories they need to fulfill the requirements. Ontario students with SHSM requirements are also tracked here.

 

Completed courses:  Provides an area where you can see all the courses that the student has complete while attending school. This is sometimes referred to as course history or transcript info.

 

Assessment results: Provides access to a student's assessment results. Assessments are centrally defined and the teachers typically record the student's results for the assessment. If results were entered for the student, they will appear here. Assessments are also sometimes referred to as school wide assessments, provincial assessments, standardized or diagnostic tests.

 

Certificates & training & awards : Provides an area where schools may choose to enter information pertaining to the student's non-academic/professional courses and awards.

 

Community involvement: (Ontario only) Provides access to the student's volunteer activities as required for graduation.

 

EQAO: (Ontario only) Provides access to the student's provincial EQAO results, if recorded for the student.

 

 

Previous years: This option has the following submenu options:

 

Promotion history: Provides a quick summary of each previous school year.

 

French/Indigenous hours: (Ontario only) Provide a summary of the student's yearly instruction in French and Indigenous languages.

 

Attendance details:  Select a school year to see attendance info from that school year.

 

Marks & comments:  Select a school year to see report card marks & comments from that year.

 

Emergency: This option has the following submenu options:

 

Contacts: Contact cards appear for all contacts that are flagged as emergency or school closure contacts. Selecting a contact card will take you directly to that contact in the Contacts option.

 

Alerts & notes: Access the student's medical alerts (based on a centrally defined table of alert types), emergency note, and dated medical notes. The emergency note appears in the summary option and as an alert icon in the profile area, for quick reference. The alert types may be set up so that they also appear in the summary and profile.  

 

Special education: Provides access to the student's special education info, if applicable. A special education icon will appear in the student's profile if the student is an active special education student.

 

Fees: Provides access to the student's fees (charges from the school to the student), payments, balance owing, and deposit info. A fees icon will appear in the student's profile if the student has a balance owing to the school.

 

Notes:: This option has the following submenu options:

 

Notepad 1, 2 and 3: (school/centrally defined title): Notepad 1, 2, and 3 are 3 distinct memo style notepad areas to record unlimited notes for the student.

 

Attendance: Attendance notes are dated notes that are intended for the recording of attendance related information.

 

Medical: Medical notes are dated notes that are intended for the recording of medical related information.

 

Dated notepad: (school/centrally defined title): This notepad allows dated notes to be entered. In addition to the note, there are 2 additional user defined list fields that may be defined for each dated note. An example setup may have a title called 'Discipline notes' with the fields 'Infraction' and 'Consequence'. Another setup may have a title called 'Guidance Meetings' with the fields called 'Reason' and 'Next steps'.

 

Special education: Special education notes are dated notes meant to record information about the student’s special education. If a student is an active special education student, a special education icon will appear in the profile.

 

Alternate program: Alternate education notes are dated notes meant to record information about the student’s alternate education programs. Student must be flagged as needing alternate programming in the Student enrolment area.

 

Custom fields: Provides a spot to record student info where there is not already a location to record that info. User defined fields may be defined centrally and by the school. Fields may be categorized so that fields appear together in a card corresponding to the category title/ Centrally defined fields may be set up as school or student specific fields. Student specific fields may only be edited by the student's edsembli record owner.

 

Incidents: Detailed info may be entered about a student's involvement in a disciplinary incident. Each incident may involve multiple students, but only this student's infraction and consequence info may be entered from the student's record.

 

Transportation: Provides access to the student's transportation method to and from school. When a student is bussed to/from school, you may record up to 2 inbound and 2 outbound buses (and a 'late' bus if provided for students involved in extra curricular activities after school).

 

Lockers: Provides access to the student's locker and lock info. Locks may be built into the lockers or the students may provide their personal locks.

 

PASI: (Alberta only) The Provincial Approach to Student Information (PASI) is an initiative by Alberta’s Ministry of Education to provide a mechanism for real-time data exchange, used to ease the process of data submission to the province.

 

Library: (only if school is licensed for edsembli's library application) Provides access to the student's current and past loan history of items from the school's library.
