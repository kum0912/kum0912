IS & e-learning for day schools

 

The IS & e-learning for day schools option provides Ontario schools and boards with the ability to generate the official Independent Study and e-Learning Register for Day School Pupils. This class-based register will include the students enrolled in classes that are set up with either the ‘Delivery’ or ‘Course Delivery type’ set to ‘Independent Study’.  

 

Schools and boards should be familiar with the official Ministry enrolment register instructions for elementary and secondary schools that are updated annually on the Ministry’s site http://www.edu.gov.on.ca/eng/policyfunding/forms.html. The principal will ensure that a system is in place to determine whether a pupil is to be enrolled as a Full-time  or a Part-time pupil, or  Independent Study (e.g. student is enrolled in classes that are to be reported on the Independent Study and e-Learning Register), as described here.

 

Enrolment in e-learning courses is normally recorded in the day school enrolment register (e.g. FT or PT registers) in the same way as enrolment in courses delivered in the classroom. The only exception is the recording of enrolment for pupils who decline to enrol in a school of the local district school board – usually pupils receiving home schooling or attending a private school. If these home schooled/private schooled pupils are receiving some instruction provided by the school they must be recorded in the Independent Study and e-Learning Register for Day School Pupils (for IS and e-learning courses) by the school that is offering the course, or in the day school register (for classroom-delivered courses).

 

A day school pupil enrolled in “independent study” is not registered for classroom instruction, but takes credit courses that fulfil the Ministry’s program and diploma requirements and meets with qualified teachers for scheduled times during the school day. It should be noted that a course cannot be considered an independent study course if any portion of it consists of classroom instructional time that is also used to classify the pupil as either “full-time” or “part-time.”

 

Full-time and part-time students may enroll in IS classes, and these students will appear on the register, however the funding (based on completed work units) may be impacted.  Some schools will set a student’s attendance type to Independent Study (in the student’s record using Enrolment > Enrolment register info), to distinguish regular full and part-time day students from those students exclusively enrolled in classes that appear on the IS & e-Learning register. Setting the attendance type to Independent Study for these students is optional (they will be sent to OnSIS as if they are part-time students).

 

Please be familiar with the Enrolment > Enrolment register info option in the student’s record. This will provide detailed information on the enrolment register(s) the student will appear on, as well as high credit and regular FTE/ADE info. Hint: on the FTE and/or ADE card in the student’s record, there’s a Details section that may be expanded to show info on how FTE/ADE is calculated, and how the student’s data impacts the register. Independent study classes are reported as ADE rather than FTE.

 

Independent Study courses are divided into a number of specific units of work that are similar in value, and these units are used to measure the pupil’s progress. Work units must be completed, not necessarily successfully, in order to be recognized for grant purposes, and the student must not exceed the number required to complete the course. This is defined during the setup of the classes that need to appear on the IS and e-Learning register.

 

Set up classes that need to appear on the IS and e-Learning register

If a class is to appear in the IS and e-Learning registers, it must be set up (Setup > Class info > Classes) as follows:

 

  • In a regular school, when adding/editing a grade 9 to 12 course, check the box “Class needs to appear on the Independent Study and e-Learning Register for Day pupils”.   
  • Set either the Delivery or Course Delivery type to Independent Study. For example, private/home schooled students would have a class that is set up as Delivery = e-Learning & Course delivery type = Independent Study; students taking an independent study class during the day may have Delivery = Day & Course delivery type = Independent Study.
  • Enter the # of work units and # of teacher contact sessions.

 

 

If the class is not scheduled in a specific period, set the minutes of instruction to reflect the average number of minutes for the teacher contact session. This value will appear on the summary page of the class register.   

 

Teachers track contact sessions and completed work units

The teacher of the class will need to track the student’s progress through the course by entering a ‘mark’ when a work unit is completed (does not need to be successfully completed to be recognized for grant purposes). Any mark other than zero will be considered a completed work unit on the register.*  

 

The teacher will have a markbook category called ‘Work Units Completed’, with markbook items Unit 1, Unit 2 … up to the # of units set up for the class.  This is an example of what the teacher will see:

The teacher may select this date and change it.  Teachers should make sure this date accurately represents the student’s completion date. For students who received full-time funding on the October and/or March count date, the student’s completion date is used to determine whether or not the work unit is included (funded) on the register. The date is also used in determining if the work unit is funded at the regular or high-credit rate based on whether the student has reached the high credit threshold on the October/March count dates).

 

Similarly, the teacher of the class will need to track contact sessions with the student by entering a ‘mark’ of A (absent), or P (Present) for each contact session throughout the duration of the course. The teacher will have a markbook category called ‘Teacher Contact Sessions’, with markbook items Session 1, Session 2 … up to the # of contact sessions set up for the class. There are times when completed units are omitted from the register because the student cannot receive funding for these units. Here are the situations:

 

  • If the student was enrolled as a full-time day school pupil on the October count date, the number of work units completed must exclude the work units completed during the period September 1 to January 31 of the current school year.
  • If the student was enrolled as a full-time day school pupil on the March count date, the number of work units completed must exclude the work units completed during the period February 1 to June 30 of the current school year.
  • If the student is enrolled as a part-time day school pupil, and is taking the same course as part of their day school instructional program, all completed work units are excluded.  

 

A summary of the student’s contact sessions and work units completed will appear on the far right of the report as shown here:

 

 

Sections labelled as A and B, as described below, are based on the teacher entries (for more info, see Teachers track contact sessions and completed work units). Pupils under 21 and over 21 (based on age as of December 31) are in separate columns.

 

Sessions scheduled:  the sum of the sessions with an A (absent) or check mark (present).

 

Sessions attended:  the sum of the sessions with a check mark (present).

 

Total number of work units completed:  the sum of the work units with a C.

 

The Regular and High-credit columns (E1 and E2) are calculated for non-adult pupils of the board who have met the over 34 non-exempt credit threshold during the time the student is taking the class. The values in these columns are best explained by examples:

 

Example 1:

 

On the October 31 and March 31 count dates, the student has not reached the 34 credit threshold (e.g. student is not high credit). This student will not show any values in column E1 and E2. This class will count as regular ADE.

 

Example 2:

 

Student is enrolled in an independent study class during the first semester, and completed it during the first semester.  The student was not high credit on October 31 but became high credit on March 31. This student will not show any values in column E1 and E2 for this class. This class will count as regular ADE.

 

Example 3:

 

Student is enrolled in a 2 credit independent study class from September 10 - June 5. The student was not high credit on October 31 (high credit factor = 0) but became high credit as of March 31 (high credit factor = .5).  The register will show 0.25 for the high credit factor (e.g. the average high credit factor (0 + .5 divided by 2).  

 

Each work unit completed by the student is dated. Using these dates, the student’s work units were completed as follows:

 

  • 10 work units completed between September 10 and October 31.

  •  20 work units completed between November 1 and March 31.

  •  6 work units completed between April 1 and June 5.  

 

The 10 work units completed on or before October 31 are all count as regular ADE.

 

The work 26 units completed after October 31 are counted partially as regular ADE and partially as high credit ADE. These are considered ‘work units when the student is high credit’ (even though the student may not have been high credit for the whole time).  

 

High-credit (column E2):  Total number of work units completed when the student was high credit X high credit factor (rounded to whole number)  

 

E2= 26 x 0.25 = 6.5 (rounded to 7)

 

Regular (column E1):  Total work units completed up to October 31 + a proportion of the work units completed when the student was high credit. Proportion = total work units completed when the student was high credit – E2.

 

E1 = 10 + (26-7) = 29

Funding - OnSIS reporting of classes on the IS and e-Learning registers

The following text is directly from the Ministry’s instructions.

 

For independent study, the average daily enrolment (ADE) will be reported through OnSIS on three submission dates – the last school day of October, March, and June. The ADE reported for each pupil should be based on the portion of work units completed by the pupil since the last OnSIS submission date.

 

The enrolment of each pupil in independent study courses must be considered under the appropriate category in the chart below for the purposes of determining a pupil’s “high-credit factor” and calculating the pupil’s ADE for independent study. The “regular” and “high-credit” ADE reported through OnSIS for each pupil should be based on the portion of work units completed by the pupil since the last OnSIS submission date.

 

 

If a pupil is admitted to a classroom-delivered day school course or program after an enrolment-count date, his or her enrolment in that course or program cannot be recorded in the Independent Study and e-Learning Register for Day School Pupils or a continuing education register.

 

The IS pupil may have been enrolled as a Full-Time day school pupil on the October and/or March enrolment-count dates. However, these pupils are not eligible for funding and their lessons/work units marked must not be included in the total number of lessons/work units submitted to OnSIS for funding purposes. For pupils enrolled as FT on the October enrolment-count date, the number of lessons/work units marked must be subtracted from the total number of lessons/work units marked during the period September 1, 2018, to January 31, 2019. Likewise, for pupils enrolled as FT on the March enrolment-count date, the number of lessons/work units marked must be subtracted from the total number of lessons/work units marked during the period February 1, 2019, to June 30, 2019.

 

ADE calculations for classes on the IS and e-Learning registers

The calculation of the average daily enrolment (ADE) for secondary pupils is split into two categories: “regular” and “high credit.” All or a portion of the credits of pupils affected by the 34-credit threshold may be identified as “high credits.” “High credits” are the credits above the 34-credit threshold that are for “nonexempt courses” taken in the current school year or semester by “non-exempt pupils.” Examples of “exempt courses” are English as a Second Language and English Literacy Development. An example of an “exempt pupil” is a pupil with an Individual Education Plan [IEP].  

 

ADE (regular and high credit) is reported to OnSIS in the Student School Enrolment batch file. The ADE reported is the sum of all independent study classes (and e-learning classes that appear on this register) that the student is enrolled in.

 

Note: the Ministry is aware that there are slight variations in the ADE reporting between OnSIS (which is by student) and the legacy EFIS report (by class).   

 

The OnSIS calculation of ADE for classes on the IS and e-Learning registers considers the sum of the credit values and completed work units for all the classes the student is taking that appear on the register:

 

Regular ADE = Credit Value x Proportion of work units completed after last count date ÷ 7.5 x (1 - high credit factor)

 

High Credit ADE = Credit Value x Proportion of work units completed after last count date ÷ 7.5 x high credit factor

 

 

Example

 

On October 31, a student has 1 regular class (1 credit) & 2 independent study classes (1 credit each), and 33 non-exempt credits in their transcripts.

 

High Credits = transcript credits + timetable credits – 34  

33 + 3 – 34 = 2 high credits

 

Enrolled Regular Credits: credits in timetable – high credits

3 – 2 = 1 regular credit

  

High Credit Factor * = number of high credits / timetable credits

2/3 = 0.67

 

* The official high credit factor calculation is a little more complex to take into account full year classes and is represented by the formula [A - (34 – B)] divided by C  (e.g.  [3 - (34-33)] divided by 3).

                                

The student has completed the following work units in each of the two independent study classes that have 20 work units each (40 total):

 

 

Work units completed by: Sept 1 - Oct 31 Nov 1 - March 31 April 1 - June 30
Class 1 (1 cr./20 units) 7 8 5
Class 2 (1 cr./20 units) 9 5

6
TOTAL 16 13 11

 

 

Proportion of work units completed during the Oct 31 submission = 16 completed units/40 total work units for both IS classes = 0.4

 

Regular ADE on the October 31 submission = credit value x proportion of work units completed during the submission / 7.5 x ( 1- high credit factor)

 

2 x 0.4 / 7.5 x (1-0.67) = 0.8/2.475 = 0.32323 student’s regular ADE

 

High Credit ADE on the October 31 submission = credit value x proportion of work units completed / 7.5 x high credit factor

 

2 x .4 / 7.5 x 0.67 = .8/ 5.025 = 0.15920 student’s high credit ADE

 

March 31 and June 30 OnSIS submissions: The student’s March ADE calculations would be based on 13/40 units completed, using the student’s March high credit factor. The student’s June ADE calculations would be based on 11/40 units completed, using the student’s March high credit factor (since there is no June high credit factor).

 

Other reports

Day schools with classes on the independent study and e-learning registers may also want to consider running the option Ontario reporting > Independent Study > Independent study & e-learning ADE.  This generates the OnSIS independent study (IS) enrolment detail and summary report showing average daily enrolment (ADE) info for the students on these class registers.
