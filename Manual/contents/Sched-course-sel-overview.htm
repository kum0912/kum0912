Overview - Course selections

After Initialize next year has been run, the school can begin the process of collecting information about the courses that the students would like to take next year.  The school will use the students' course selections to schedule classes and to create the students’ new timetables for the new year.   

 

Before course selections are entered, the school updates the list of courses available next school year.  New courses may need to be added by the board/division/district first, if the course is to included in the student's completed courses (transcript).

 

Students can enter their course selections, or the school may enter them individually or in bulk for groups of students with the same courses.  Once entered, course selections may be tallied and analyzed. If the school determines that a course is to be cancelled, there are tools to assist in changing the courses of the affected students.

Each course that a student selects may be either a preferred course or an alternate course:

 

• Preferred  courses:  These are the courses that the student would prefer to have in their timetable next year.

• Alternate courses:  These are the courses that the student would like to take if unable to get into a preferred course.

 

Each preferred course has Y/N field for “Can this course be substituted with an alternate?”.  The automatic process for creating the students’ timetables (called Load students) has an option to allow automatic replacement of a preferred courses with an alternate course rather than leave the student with a conflict in their timetable. There may be some reports that refer to Mandatory, Elective, or Alternate choices. Choices (aka course selections) that have N to this question (no they can't be substituted) may be referred to as Mandatory, and if they have Y to this question, they may be referred to as Elective.

 

If the school grants students access to edit course selections online, the school may optionally lock specific courses so that they may not be removed by the student.  Course selections are available to only those students attending the school next year (school year status is either ‘this year and next year’ or ‘next year only’).

 

These are the recommended steps for collecting and analyzing the students’ course selections:

  

1. Courses:  Update the list of courses that will be available to the students next year.  Add new courses and remove courses that are no longer offered.  Every course has a Y/N setting for ‘Display for student use during course selection?’ so that the school may use courses for their own internal purposes.  See Scheduling next year > Course selections > Courses for details on this step.

 

2. Student course selections:  Rather than entering the course selections for the students, often the students record their own selections using edsembli CONNECT for students. Other schools use third party career planning applications where students record their selections and these selections are automatically imported into the SIS.

 

A student’s course selections may be edited by the office using the Course selections option in the student’s record or by selecting Scheduling next year > Course selections > Individual student.  

 

Tools exists to automatically add or remove a course selection for a group of students, or to replace one course with a different course.  See Scheduling next year > Course selections > Add/delete/replace course selections for details on this tool.

 

3. Analyze course selections: Once the course selections are added for the students, they may be analyzed as follows:

 

Scheduling next year > Course selections > Course tally:  Use this report to list the courses along with the number of students who have selected the course as one of their preferred courses.  The course tally will help identify courses that may have insufficient interest from the students that the course will not be offered.  

 

Scheduling next year > Course selections > Course analysis by grade:  Use this report to list the courses along with the number of students who have selected the course.  This report will help identify students who have selected courses outside their grade level.  

 

Scheduling next year > Course selections > Invalid prerequisites: Use this report to identify students who have selected a course but the student does not have the prerequisite courses.

 

Scheduling next year > Course selections >List course selections:  Use this report to see the number of course selections have been entered for each student.  This report will help identify students with too many or too few courses.  The report also includes the actual course codes entered for each student.  

 

4. Counsel students if cancelling courses: Use Scheduling next year > Course selections > Counselling list to see the names of the students who have requested each course.  This report may be used by the school if a course has insufficient interest that it will not be offered next year and the guidance staff need to counsel the affected students.  The report also provides the ability to jump directly into the student’s record so that the course selections can be modified.
