Student records > Fees

 

Overview

Every school collects money (funds) from students for various reasons. Funds include all forms of payment, from cash to cheque to e-payments.  How these funds are collected and recorded may vary by school. edsembli provides the tools to track student Fees.

 

The edsembli fees area is an excellent time saving feature that allows schools to record financial transactions for both current and past students. It has been designed as an Accounts Receivable system for students, and beyond data entry, there are powerful reporting features. Schools are strongly encouraged to involve their financial resources during the set up process.

 

Fees are school-specific, however, with permissions, you may see the fees recorded by another school.

 

Every student can have fees and payments tracked. The school creates a list of fee types to broadly describe the type of fee.  Examples might include course fee, bus fee, student activity fee, lock purchase.  When a fee is applied to a student, or group of students, the type of fee is selected and an additional comment may be recorded if necessary to help clarify the fee. As payments are made to the student’s account, the payment is associated with the fee to track how much the student still owes.

 

Students may also have funds that are submitted to the school for deposits on items they are borrowing from the school.  For example, some schools require text book deposit funds that are collected at the start of the course and the funds are returned to the student when they return the text book to the school.  These student deposits may also be tracked in edsembli SIS. The school creates a list of deposit fee types to track the required deposits.  The payment of the funds by the student, and the subsequent return of those funds are all tracked using the DEPOSITS tab in the student’s fee area.  If no deposit fee types are defined by the school, the DEPOSITS tab is unavailable.

 

Students with outstanding account balances will have a fees icon in their profile area.  Hovering over the icon will show the student’s account balance (e.g. Account balance: $25.00).  If the student owes money for both fees and deposits, the sum of both of these will appear followed by a breakdown (e.g. Account balance: $35.00 ($25.00 fees + $10.00 deposits). This balance will also prominently display above the student’s fee information when in that area of the student’s record, so you see the overall balance regardless of whether you are looking at only fees or only deposits.

 

In this example the student has an Account balance credit of $18.00, with nothing overdue.

 

  

In addition to student fees and deposits, some schools may want to track other school funds that are neither student-based nor associated to students. This might include money submitted to the office for a school fund raiser, or a corporate donation to the school, or money collected from the vending machine. These funds are simply recorded for record-keeping purposes only; no records are kept with respect to what happens to the funds after they are collected.

Summary tab

 

When looking at a student’s fees, this is a sample summary view (SUMMARY tab) as shown here:

 

  

The summary view will be different for each student, depending upon the status of the student’s accounts with the school.  The summary may include up to 3 cards to convey the student’s account status:

 

  • Account balance card (every student will have this)
  • Unpaid fees card (appears only if the student has unpaid fees)
  • Payment credits card (appears only if the student has payment credits – money not associated with any fees).

 

Note: the information in the summary may include references to due dates.  Schools choose to track due dates on every fee.  When a fee is not paid by the due date, it is considered overdue. If a school does not track due dates, any area of fees references to due dates or overdue unpaid fees will be disabled.

 

Account balance

Every student will have an Account balance card on the SUMMARY tab. The student’s account balance will be displayed in the app bar. The student’s account balance represents the amount of money the student owes the school.  This may be due to unpaid fees or deposits required by the school.  The student’s account balance is displayed above the student’s fee information as shown below, so you see the overall balance regardless of whether you are looking at only fees or only deposits.  If no deposits money is unpaid, it will just show how much the student owes.  In this example, the student has $15.00 in outstanding fees:

Unpaid fees

The Unpaid fees card will only appear if the student has fees that are not yet paid (i.e. outstanding fees).  The card will list the student’s fees with balances greater than zero (e.g. unpaid fees).

Payment credits

The card titled Payment credits (shown below) will only appear if the student has money in the account, but that money is not applied to any fee. This typically only happens if payments were made prior to the fees being applied to the student, a course fee was returned, or an overpayment of a fee was made so there is extra money in the student’s account.

 

 

See Payment credits for more information on how to apply the payment credit to outstanding fees.

See Refunds for more on how to give a refund all or part of the payment.

 

 

Deposits tab

If the school has set up deposit fee types, every student will have access to a DEPOSITS tab, where deposit information can be tracked. This includes adding a deposit fee (to indicate that a deposit is required), making a deposit payment (when the deposit funds are submitted), and refunding the deposit (when the deposit is no longer required).

 

See Deposits in the Fees section of the manual.

 

Deposit balance card

The student’s deposit account balance will be displayed in the tool bar. In this example, the student has not yet paid the $12 deposit and has a Deposit balance of $12.00 in outstanding deposits to pay.

Deposits card

The Deposits card shows the deposit history including Date, Due, Fee type, Invoice number, Deposit required (amount), Payment (if any), Balance (if any) and Comments (if any).

 

Row clicking a deposit lets you view Details, Add, Edit and Refund a deposit, as well as convert the deposit in to a credit if a refund is not wanted.
