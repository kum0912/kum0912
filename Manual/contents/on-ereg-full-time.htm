Enrolment registers - Full-time

 

The full-time option provides Ontario schools and boards with the ability to generate the official enrolment registers for full-time students, and unofficial register reports for school/board use.  

 

Schools and boards should be familiar with the official Ministry enrolment register instructions for elementary and secondary schools that are updated annually on the Ministry’s site http://www.edu.gov.on.ca/eng/policyfunding/forms.htmll. The principal will ensure that a system is in place to determine whether a pupil is to be enrolled as a Full-Time (described here) or a Part-Time pupil, or as a secondary pupil engaged in Independent Study.

 

In general, a pupil’s enrolment status is based on the number of minutes a pupil is “registered for classroom instruction” per school day. A full-time pupil is typically registered for 210 minutes or more of classroom instruction per school day. The term “registered for classroom instruction” refers to a pupil who is enrolled for classroom instruction and who is attending classes regularly. The amount of time specified for classroom instruction in each case is the average amount of time in the school cycle that is allocated in the pupil’s timetable to the classes that the pupil is expected to attend. See the Ministry’s instructions for details on time recognized or not recognized as classroom instructional time.

 

Full-time pupils attending elementary and secondary schools include:

  

  • A pupil who is registered for classroom instruction for an average of 210 minutes or more per school day in the school cycle  
  • A pupil aged 14–17 who has a Supervised Alternative Learning Plan (SALP) that provides for an average of at least 70 minutes of instruction per school day in which the pupil may earn a credit  
  • A pupil who is participating in an Intensive Behavioural Intervention program, regardless of the amount of classroom instructional time for which he or she is registered
  • A secondary pupil who is enrolled in a day school that has a school year calendar (12 month calendar under Regulation 304), in which the pupil is eligible to earn at least 7 credits during the school year.

 

The full-time register reports will rely on the information recorded for the students. Please be familiar with the Enrolment > Enrolment register info option in the student’s record. This will provide detailed information on the enrolment register(s) the student will appear on, as well as high credit and regular FTE/ADE info. Hint: on the FTE and/or ADE card in the student’s record, there’s a Details section that may be expanded to show info on how FTE/ADE is calculated, and how the student’s data impacts the register. Typically, full-time students are (or were) recorded as full-time in the attendance type field, at some point this school year.

 

To access the full-time register reports, choose Ontario reporting > Enrolment registers > Full-time.

 

 

Select GO on the Full-time card and you will see a form like this:

 

 

There are 3 official registers formats for full-time students as per the Ministry’s instructions. Use the tabs to navigate between the 3 formats. It will default the DETAILS tab.

 

 

DETAILS - generates the Enrolment Details register for full-time pupils.

 

MONTHLY SUMMARY – generates the Monthly Enrolment Summary for full-time pupils.

 

SUMMARY (FTE) - generates the Enrolment Summary with regular and high-credit FTE data. This register is required for full-time secondary pupils who are under 21 years of age.  This option applies to schools with students in grades 9 or higher.

 

All tabs provide similar functionality for generating the reports.  First set the options on the page (as detailed below), then select the Print button located at the bottom of the form. Once the report has been submitted to the task queue, you may leave this application or generate other reports. You will receive notification when your report is ready. Reports are saved in file manager under Provincial > Enrolment registers.

 

DETAILS tab

The DETAILS tab will generate the official full-time enrolment details registers. Each of the following grade groups has a distinct register:

 

  • Junior kindergarten
  • Kindergarten
  • Grades 1 to 3
  • Grades 4 to 8
  • Secondary students (Grades 9 to 12)

 

The grade groups available will depend upon the grades offered at the school. The board will have access to all groups. Select the grade groups to be printed or select the Print all registers option (one register per group will be printed).

 

 

 

Typically, the registers are printed Alphabetically, but schools may opt to print it by homeroom (referred to as an organizational group in the Ministry’s instructions).

 

 

Here is an example from the Ministry’s instructions. An Adult column will appear for the secondary students (grade 9-12 group). An adult student is a pupil who is already 21 years of age or over or who will turn 21 on or before December 31.  The ‘OP’ column will show OP if the student is considered an Other Pupil (OP). OP will appear if the board defined student board residence status does not use ministry code 01, 06, 07, or 09.

 

 

The Unofficial option will provide the same register report, however, the students are not in the official grade groups.  For example, all the students in the school, who were full-time at some point, may be printed in an unofficial register.  The standard print methods are available, but the “Alphabet” option is defaulted.

 

 

For elementary schools, the unofficial reports will reference “All Grades” rather than an official grade group.

SUMMARY (FTE) tab

The SUMMARY (FTE) tab will generate the Ministry’s enrolment summary with regular and high-credit data for full-time students in grades 9-12. When the SUMMARY (FTE) tab is selected, the form will look like this:  

 

 

This register is typically printed for the entire school, but there is the option to print just for a group of students (alphabet order or other orders), and there is the option to print by homeroom.  There are distinct reports for school aged students and adult students.

 

The student’s FTE info as of 2 count dates (typically the last school day in October and March).
