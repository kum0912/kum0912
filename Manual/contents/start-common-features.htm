Google’s Material Design Principles

edsembli SIS has taken advantage of all the research and design standards developed by Google’s Material Design. They have design standards for everything: colour schemes, fonts, writing styles, layout, tabs, buttons, fields, tables, their animated floating action buttons (FABs), and so much more. If you look at their design specifications at https://www.google.com/design/spec/material-design/introduction.html, you’ll quickly see how much effort they have put into these specifications (check out the Components section of their specifications to see the depth of detail). By using Material Design standards, you will find the application is intuitive and follows many web design standards that you will likely already be familiar with.

 

Material Design has taken into account many design aspects that make an application user accessible. Since Material Design uses a primary colour and an accent colour, as you change your theme colours, the application automatically responds by adjusting text colours from white to black depending upon the contrast ratio between the text colour and its background. As well, touch targets such as buttons, icons, menu options, etc. allow users with lower motor dexterity, and those who aren’t able to see the screen, to tap on the correct targets. The built in extra padding ensures that the application is accessible for all users.

 

Please take a moment to read our Accessibility section, even if accessibility is not something that you have concern with. For example, if your monitor setting is too bright, you may have difficulty using the application for extended periods, or if you would prefer to see more content by shrinking everything, you may reduce your browser’s zoom setting from 100%.

Mouse, keyboard, and touch friendly

Depending on the device you are using, you may have access to a keyboard and a mouse, or just a touch screen. Rather than referring to click/touch throughout this document, we will refer to the term “select” to represent either clicking or touching a particular area on the application. If the selected area requires input from a keyboard, and a keyboard is not available, the screen will automatically display a keyboard for the entry of letters and/or numbers and symbols.

App bar

When you log in to edsembli SIS,  you will see the dashboard page. The dashboard page has 2 sections:

 

  1. The “app bar” (application bar) shown in dark blue below. This is where you will find actions that can be performed that impact the entire page.
  2. The “content” shown as the white area below, will reflect the information that is appropriate for the page. In this example, the content is empty as each user may have different content appearing. The content of each page is explained in depth in other sections of the manual.

 

 

Your security access determines what school(s) and what role(s) are applicable, and what your default school and role will be upon login. It also determines what actions are available from the app bar.

 

Everyone has access to a Menu icon from the app bar.

 

As you hover over an icon in the app bar, a tool tip will appear to help explain the purpose of the icon. Selecting this icon will activate the main menu to allow you to navigate to different parts of the application (see Navigation for more info).

 

If you have access to multiple schools, you will have a Change school icon (drop down arrow). This is also where you can select the board/division/district (aka central office). The central office is responsible for much of the setup of the SIS for those features that are common between all schools (e.g. setup tables).

 

 

In the app bar on every page, there will be a help icon. Select this icon to open a new browser window with the help content specific to that page.

 

 

Those with security rights may also create help content specific to the board/division/district.  Those with rights to create help will see the second icon with the + sign (tool tip will say Add your help).  Select the icon to create your own help content that is shared with all schools in your board/division/district. Once your help is created, all users will have a Your help icon (it's the same but without the + in the icon). Your help is specific to each page in the application allowing boards to add additional help content everywhere additional help is needed specifically for your users.

 

Your account and role information can be viewed, and changed if you have multiple roles assigned to you, using the icon shown here to switch between your roles:

 

 

Your role has a significant influence on what is available in the application.

 

Here are the role possibilities:

 

  • School staff – provides access to the functionality that the school office and administrative staff need.
  • Central office – provides access to examine the data from multiple schools and provides tools for those who are responsible for maintaining edsembli SIS.
  • Teacher – provides access to the functionality that teachers need.
  • Student – provides access to the functionality that students are granted.
  • Parent/Guardian – provides access to the functionality that parents are granted.

 

If you are a teacher at a school, and you also are a parent/guardian of student attending a school in the board/division/district, you can log in once and change roles as needed without logging out each time.

 

To the left of role icon, you will find a count-down of the time remaining in the current session (e.g. 15 minutes and 40 seconds in the example below)  and the Your notifications icon explained further below.  If there is not activity on the page, the user will be automatically signed out when the counter reaches zero.  The counter is reset as you navigate to different forms (aka interfaces, web pages), or when read/write processes are preformed (e.g. save, refresh).  

 

 

In the top right hand corner of the application (in the app bar), you will find the More options icon. When you are finished using edsembli SIS, select this icon then select Sign out. Use the My account option to set your interface language or to change your password. The Support information option provides info about who to contact if questions (this is configurable so that board/division/district contact info can be appear here). Navigation

Navigation is the movement between different parts of the application. You will find that you can easily figure out how to move through edsembli SIS with ease. There are many design features that facilitate navigation, some of which are described next.

When the down arrow is available, there are 2 possible actions:

 

  • Clicking the top level menu option (e.g. Attendance). This will open the attendance page with all the submenu options and the corresponding cards found in each submenu.
  • Clicking the down arrow will expand the menu tree to show the submenu options. Clicking a submenu option (e.g. Enter attendance) will open the page with only that submenu expanded as shown below.  

 

 

Toggle between showing and hiding the cards by clicking anywhere on the line with the submenu title or select the Show more and Show less icons. Cards provide an brief explanation of the functionality of each option.  Select a button on the bottom of the card to access the function (e.g. click GO button).  

 

In addition to the Main menu icon, you will find slight variations of the menu icon in other areas of the application. These icons will show/hide a menu corresponding to the specific area of the application you are working in. For example, when working in a student’s record on a larger device, the student record menu is visible but it may be hidden on a smaller device.  Select the Hide menu/Show menu icon to hide or show the menu if needed.

 

 

 

Tabs

Tabs make navigating between different views or categorizations of data easy. The selected tab will be emphasized and underlined in your accent colour. Select another tab option to navigate to it, or select the arrows on the right/left, if applicable.

Some pages have only app bars (where the help icon is located) and some pages have one or more tool bars with icons specific to the data presented in the area below the tool bar.  Here is an attendance  tool bar with icons specific to that attendance functionality. The tool bar and the contents below the tool bar are sometime collectively referred to as a card (e.g. the attendance card).

 

 

 

Tool bars always contain a Hide all or Show more icon that can be used to collapse (hide the contents area) or expand (show the contents area).

 

 

 

Some icons when selected will present multiple options like this example which  will change the display of the contents (e.g. showing the data in list, card or table view). The tool bar will show the icon of the selected view but selecting the icon will again present the different options.

Other pages use a bottom bar that includes buttons such as CANCEL, SAVE,  or CLOSE.  Note: SAVE buttons are disabled if there is no data to save (e.g. if you navigate between tabs it may save data automatically).

Yes/no switches

Yes/no settings may appear as check boxes or as switches. By selecting the switch, the setting will toggle between yes/no settings (e.g. first time selected will set it to Yes, second time selected will set it to No). Switches just need to be selected (clicked or touched, not dragged to the right or left). If set to Yes, it will appear in the accent colour. No will appear disabled.

Your notifications (Task queue and file manager for printing and long running processes)

In an effort to reduce paper, printing is becoming less prominent as schools attempt to be paperless. Most print actions are available by selecting the More icon, or the Print FAB button if generating a report is the main functionality on a form. When a print or long running processing action is made, the report (or process) is submitted to the task queue. Once it has been submitted, you may leave the application or generate other reports.

 

In the app bar there is an icon called Your notifications.  If a red number appears in the icon, it means that you have notifications. Selecting the icon will open the task queue where you may monitor the progress of any tasks submitted to the task queue (e.g. print jobs or long running processes like transfer to current at the end of the school year). Each task typically goes through 3 steps, with each step generating a notification: Submitted, Processing, Completed.

 

 

Select the icon to see the notifications as shown below:

 

 

Select a completed report to access the download option.

 

 

 

Unless the report is deleted, it will be available for later reference in File manager. File manager and Task queue are also available from the Main menu.

Browser caching

The browser cache is a temporary storage location on your device where the browser keeps things that it downloaded once in case they’re needed again. Each browser may have a different method for clearing the cache, and if instructed to do so, you can search the internet for instructions specific to your browser.
